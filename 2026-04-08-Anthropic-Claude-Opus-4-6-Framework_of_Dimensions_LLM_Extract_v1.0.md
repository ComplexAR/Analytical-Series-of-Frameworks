## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Dimensions framework identifies all relevant dimensions (axes of variation) across an 18-tier ontology—from substrate physics through cognitive, social, symbolic, normative, technological, and meta-systemic tiers—to prevent reductionism and category errors in problem analysis. It maps which tiers are relevant to a focal phenomenon, characterizes the salient dimensions within each tier, identifies cross-tier relationships (emergence, downward causation, reflexivity), and surfaces where dimensional distortion or omission creates Hiddens. It populates the Evidence Ledger, Hiddens Register, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with completeness checks and tier-level intervention guidance.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reductionism | Analysis collapses to single tier; higher-order properties denied or treated as epiphenomenal; intervention assumes tier independence. | Pressure toward simplicity; dominant paradigm privileges one tier; measurement/incentives flatten dimensional space; silo'd expertise. | Misdiagnosis; ineffective interventions; reflexive effects missed; emergent phenomena invisible. |
| Dimensional Distortion | Measurement or incentive systems systematically bias dimensional profiles; some dimensions over-weighted, others suppressed. | Metrics are selected for convenience, not completeness; power actors prefer certain dimensions; ontologies embed preferred assumptions. | Decisions driven by biased dimensional picture; alternative solutions invisible; real dynamics hidden. |
| Tier Neglect | Relevant tier is outside analyst's awareness or expertise; phenomena at that tier are not modelled or monitored. | Disciplinary silos; expertise bottlenecks; paradigm boundaries; complexity overwhelms attention. | Surprises at neglected tiers; interventions miss leverage points; long-lag effects undetected. |
| Cross-Tier Decoupling | Tiers are analyzed independently; cross-tier relationships (emergence, feedback, downward causation) are unmapped or denied. | Disciplinary boundaries reinforce tier isolation; complex interactions hard to model; expertise is tier-specific. | High-consequence effects missed; intervention cascades unpredicted; reflexive loops unmapped. |
| Emergent Suppression | New dimensions emerge (or become relevant) but are not named or integrated into analysis; emergent phenomena treated as noise. | Novel phenomena don't fit existing dimensional schemas; naming requires paradigm shift; inertia resists integration. | Blind spots to emerging phenomena; late response to material changes; competitive/institutional disadvantage. |
| Normative-Factual Collapse | Normative dimensions (values, obligations) are smuggled into ostensibly factual analysis; or factual tiers are stripped of normative loading. | Analyst conflates is/ought; power actors hide value choices; paradigms treat values as objective facts. | Unspoken disagreement on normative tier; false consensus; covert value imposition; legitimacy deficits. |
| Reflexive Blindness | Analysis ignores that dimensional frameworks themselves shape what is observed; measurement and conceptualization are reflexive. | Framework treated as neutral mirror of reality; observation assumed independent of observer; tools treated as transparent. | Unexamined framework bias; paradigm assumptions hidden; alternative dimensional schemas foreclosed. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What dimensions are salient/invisible from common positions; what dimensional variation is below the attention threshold? |
| II (Systemic) | How do lower tiers couple with higher tiers; what cross-tier feedback loops and emergence mechanisms are unmapped? |
| III (Informational) | What dimensions are measurable/unmeasurable; what evidence gaps exist; what measurement systems distort dimensional profiles? |
| IV (Temporal) | What dimensions operate at different timescales; what slow-moving or long-lag dimensions are invisible on short horizons? |
| V (Relational) | Whose preferred dimensions are emphasised; whose dimensional ontologies are suppressed; what power asymmetries hide alternative dimensional analyses? |
| VI (Ontological) | What dimensional categories don't yet exist or are not conceivable; what emergent dimensions are being named/integrated; what is impossible to represent in current schema? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Reductionism (higher tiers reduced to or denied in favor of lower tiers)
- Dimensional Distortion (measurement/incentive systems bias dimensional profiles)
- Tier Neglect (relevant tiers outside awareness or expertise)
- Cross-Tier Decoupling (relationships between tiers are unmapped)
- Emergent Suppression (novel dimensions not integrated into analysis)
- Normative-Factual Collapse (values smuggled into facts or vice versa)
- Reflexive Blindness (dimensional frameworks treated as transparent rather than constitutive)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reductionist analysis or interventions failing | Framework of Systems; Framework of Causation | Multi-tier mapping; cross-tier dependency analysis | Re-analyze at multiple tiers; identify cross-tier mechanisms; redesign interventions. |
| Measurement gaming or metric distortion | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Metrics | Dimensional completeness audit; metric definition review | Expand metrics to include suppressed dimensions; add leading indicators for higher tiers. |
| Emergent phenomena not named or integrated | Framework of Hiddens (Tier 2); Framework of Learning | Novel dimension naming; schema evolution | Name emerging dimensions; test dimensional relevance; integrate into framework. |
| Normative tier suppressed or denied | Framework of Values; Framework of Legitimacy | Value and legitimacy audit; normative tier explicit inclusion | Make normative dimensions explicit; map stakeholder value variance; design legitimacy work. |
| Cross-tier reflexive effects unpredicted | Framework of Systems; Framework of Learning | Reflexive effect mapping; feedback loop analysis | Model reflexive loops; run scenario analysis on cross-tier effects; test predictions. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify focal phenomenon/problem; establish system boundary and time horizon.
2. List anticipated relevant tiers (use T0–T5 default for technical systems; T0–T8 for socio-technical; T0–T12 for social/policy domains).
3. For each focal tier, sketch 3–5 salient dimensions and operational indicators (what varies, how is it measured).
4. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six Hiddens meta-categories; flag obvious omissions (neglected tiers, distorted dimensions, suppressed values).
5. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence dimensional gaps or distortions; note if tier neglect or reductionism is suspected; flag for escalation.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Tier relevance map: list all 18 tiers (T0–T17); for each, assess relevance to focal phenomenon (in-scope, out-of-scope, uncertain); note rationale for excluded tiers.
2. Dimensional profiles: for each in-scope tier, identify salient dimensions, operational indicators (measurable quantities or observable states), values/ranges, measurement quality, and data gaps.
3. Cross-tier relationship map: sketch emergence pathways (how higher tiers arise from lower), downward causation (how higher tiers reshape lower), and reflexive loops (where analysis and intervention loop back).
4. Transversal axes: assess complexity, information (data richness), value/normativity, temporal (timescales), spatial/scalar (geographic/organisational scale) across all tiers; identify axis-driven synthesis opportunities.
5. Distortion audit: examine measurement systems, incentive structures, power distributions; identify which dimensions are over-weighted, which suppressed; flag normative-factual collapse.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence dimensional gap or distortion, identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; propose dimensional completeness tests.
7. Limits register: document what cannot be dimensionalized, why, and what phenomena are emergent-only; record reflexive assumptions in framework itself.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add dimensional Hiddens with meta-category tag (Perceptual, Systemic, Informational, Temporal, Relational, Ontological); mechanism; consequence. | After Tier 1 scan; escalate high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hiddens. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record dimensional profiles, cross-tier hypotheses, measurement quality, distortion analysis; note gaps (missing data for neglected tiers, measurement system bias). | After dimensional profiling and distortion audit; flag unmeasurable dimensions. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about tier relevance, cross-tier mechanisms, dimensional completeness; propose tests (measurement expansion, alternative frameworks, reflexivity probes). | Identified Unknowns and dimensional gaps; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request evidence for neglected tiers; request measurement systems and incentive audits; request alternative dimensional analyses from different disciplines/perspectives. | To reduce tier/dimensional Unknowns; coordinate with Evidence and Hiddens. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified all in-scope tiers, profiled salient dimensions per tier, mapped cross-tier relationships, audited measurement/incentive distortions, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and identified dimensional completeness requirements for decision-critical phenomena?
