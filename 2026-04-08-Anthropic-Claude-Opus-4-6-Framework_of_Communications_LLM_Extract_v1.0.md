## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Communications framework maps how information moves (and fails to move) through channels, encodings, interaction structures, and governance protocols; classifies communication patterns into six meta-categories and thirty core types; profiles flows along five cross-cutting dimensions; and surfaces the four systematic sources of communication-based visibility failure (channel blindspots, encoding collapse, protocol gaps, feedback failure). It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with communication-specific blindnesses and remediation requirements, enabling downstream frameworks to design corrective governance and intervention.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amplification | Some signals are repeated/rebroadcasted across channels; others suppressed or ignored. | Social amplification (attractive ideas spread); network effects (hubs amplify signals); incentive-driven amplification (rewarded messages repeated). | Organisational "truth" determined by amplification bias not actual importance; power and dissent suppression. |
| Decay | Signals degrade, lose detail, or lose credibility as they propagate. | Relay distortion (each step rewrites); forgetting (older signals fade); version proliferation (multiple "truths" circulate). | Decisions detached from ground truth; latency creates stale models; information loss accumulates. |
| Drift | Shared understanding diverges over time without explicit conflict; parties operate on different models unknowingly. | Decoupled evolution (groups interpret same signal differently); temporal compression (context fades); absent correction loops. | Coordinated action becomes impossible; decisions by one group invalidate another's; cascade risk grows. |
| Feedback Correction | Errors detected and corrected propagate back to source. | Error detection (gap between intended/actual); correction transmission (fix sent to source); correction acceptance (source acts). | Weak feedback = compounding errors; strong feedback = resilience and learning capacity. |
| Shadowing | Important communication shifts from formal to informal; governance loses visibility. | Formal channel friction (slow, gatekeeping); social opportunity cost; incentive asymmetry (informal rewarded, formal punished). | Governance becomes fiction; real decisions unmapped; coordination costs rise; cascade risk when shadow fails. |
| Encoding Loss | Details, context, or nuance lost as information encoded for transmission. | Abstraction (detail collapsed); format rigidity (rich context squeezed into fixed fields); lossy compression. | Critical context hidden; decisions on incomplete information; misunderstandings compound when context missing. |
| Authority Asymmetry | Information flows more easily in one direction based on power/status; upward filters for "good news." | Power dynamics (subordinates filter for boss's preferences); risk asymmetry (bearing cost of bad news); gatekeeping. | Leadership operates on filtered reality; field understands strategy but doesn't correct it; drift compounds. |
| Legitimacy Crisis | Communications from certain sources/channels no longer trusted; audience discounts message regardless of content. | Betrayed expectations (repeated failures); power asymmetry (audience believes self-interest); manipulation exposure. | Messages from delegitimised source ignored or inverted; communication becomes dysfunctional. |
| Correction Lag | Time between error creation and correction too long; decisions made before correction arrives. | Detection latency (error unnoticed for days/weeks); transmission latency (correction propagates slowly); processing latency. | Errors compound; damage larger than if correction arrived faster; learning is slow. |
| Cascading Silence | Suppression of one channel triggers suppression of others; self-reinforcing silence. | Fear contagion (punishment triggers self-silence); network effects (critical mass silence); social proof (silence indicates agreement). | Silence becomes complete; dissent/error reporting vanish; vulnerability grows unseen. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What channels are missing, slow, or inaccessible; what information has no path to decision-makers; what is the organisation structurally blind to? |
| II (Systemic) | What encodings hide context, nuance, or tail events; what is the lossy compression in each representation; what would users see if they saw the raw data instead? |
| III (Informational) | What protocol-practice gaps exist; when do staff bypass formal procedures and why; where is the disconnect between procedure and actual work? |
| IV (Temporal) | What feedback loops are broken, slow, or asymmetric; how long does error detection and correction take; is feedback reaching the original source? |
| V (Relational) | Who controls information flow and access; whose voice is amplified and whose is suppressed; who decides what gets communicated and to whom? |
| VI (Ontological) | What categories, groups, or information types are outside the communication system entirely; who cannot be represented in the channels available; what alternatives are not conceivable? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Channel Blindspots (information with no available path to decision-makers; structural gaps in channel coverage)
- Encoding Collapse (context, nuance, uncertainty hidden in standard encodings; lossy compression in abstractions)
- Protocol-Practice Gaps (formal procedures differ from actual work; exceptions become routine; incentive-driven non-compliance)
- Feedback Failure (errors undetected or uncorrected; correction loops slow or asymmetric; lack of confirmation of understanding)
- Signal Filtering & Amplification (selective relay; information distorted through cascade; some signals amplified, others suppressed)
- Strategic Silence & Information Withholding (deliberate non-communication; need-to-know restrictions; invisibility through deliberate omission)
- Translation & Format Loss (meaning distorted crossing encoding boundaries; interpretation diverges at system/organisational boundaries)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Channel coverage gaps or shadow networks | Framework of Governance; Framework of Systems | Channel audit and inventory; escalation path mapping | Audit formal/informal channels; reduce friction with formal channels; legitimise working shadow channels with governance. |
| Encoding/representation asymmetry | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Narratives | Multi-encoding audit; interpretation testing across users | Use multiple encodings for critical signals; preserve raw data alongside summaries; annotate what each encoding hides. |
| Protocol-practice divergence | Framework of Governance; Framework of Institutions | Protocol audit; actual practice observation | Align formal procedures with actual work; reduce perverse incentives in protocol; address exceptions formally. |
| Feedback loop failure or latency | Framework of Systems; Framework of Learning & Adaptation | Correction cycle timing; feedback completeness audit | Establish feedback loops at each interface; reduce latency between error and correction; make understanding confirmation mandatory. |
| Authority-driven filtering or distortion | Framework of Power; Framework of Responsibility | Authority structure audit; source credibility analysis | Establish protected channels for bad news; diversify information sources; reduce risk for messengers; rebuild trust. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Map focal channels in play (formal and informal); identify who communicates what to whom; flag obvious gaps or shadows.
2. Classify 2–4 critical communication types into meta-categories I–VI; note which types are active and which gaps exist.
3. Assess two critical dimensions for focal channels: Signal-to-Noise (is the channel clear or noisy?) and Latency (how fast does information move?).
4. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan (30–60 min): touch all four Hiddens sources lightly; identify which sources (channel blindspots, encoding collapse, protocol gaps, feedback failure) are most visible/critical.
5. Shortlist the 2–3 highest-consequence Hiddens from Tier 1; flag detection/remediation interfaces; escalate if high-consequence and high-uncertainty.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Create channel inventory (formal + informal + shadow); map flow volume, latency, users, access controls, governance; identify channel-specific failure rates.
2. Classify all focal communication types (meta-category + core type 1–30); profile dimension values for each type: Multiplicity, Signal-to-Noise, Latency, Correctness & Understanding, Governability.
3. Analyse all ten dynamics patterns; for each focal channel/type, assess which dynamics are active (nascent, present, high-risk) and why; identify cascades and feedback loops.
4. Map three interface types: Channel Interfaces (where signals transition across media), Translation Interfaces (where encodings change), Authority Interfaces (where hierarchy/legitimacy shifts interpretation).
5. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each of four sources (channel blindspots, encoding, protocol, feedback), identify specific mechanisms, detectability, consequence, and remediation levers; populate Hiddens Register.
6. Assess which Targeted Scans (OG §7.5) apply: signal filtering, strategic silence, distortion & relay, translation loss, amplification bias, temporal drift, authority capture.
7. Document residual Unknowns; apply Escalation Rule if any Unknown could change decisions or accountability; generate closure artefacts (Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, Hypothesis/Test Backlog, Information Requests).

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add communication-specific Hiddens with source category (channel/encoding/protocol/feedback), mechanism, where it manifests, detectability, consequence. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record channel inventory, type classifications, dimensional assessments, dynamic pattern presence/absence, evidence quality, gaps. | After channel mapping and type classification; note gaps (e.g., latency measurements, understanding confirmation testing). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about communication effects, bottlenecks, feedback loops, protocol compliance; propose tests (channel audits, latency measurement, comprehension testing, feedback cycle testing). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request channel logs, flow data, timing metrics, stakeholder interviews on "how does info really move?", protocol documentation, feedback confirmation records. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Evidence and Governance frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified focal channels (formal + informal), classified critical communication types into meta-categories I–VI, assessed key dimensions (Latency, Signal-to-Noise, Correctness), run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan across all four sources, and escalated any high-consequence Unknowns to appropriate frameworks (Hiddens, Governance, Evidence)?

## Section 8: Cross-Framework Routing Pointers

### 8a. Upstream Inputs (frameworks commonly feeding into this one)

- Situations & Context (what situation type demands what communication pattern?)
- Agents & Personas (who are the communicators; what are their knowledge, incentives, capabilities?)
- Evidence (what evidence about communication effectiveness, failures, feedback quality exists?)
- Power & Incentives (who controls communication; what incentives drive speech or silence?)
- Culture & Norms (what is acceptable to say; what is taboo; what is the culture of error reporting?)
- Boundaries & Scale (at what boundaries does communication break; what scale dynamics apply?)
- Systems (what feedback and control loops are embedded in communication architecture?)

### 8b. Downstream Handoffs (frameworks commonly consuming this framework's outputs)

- Framework of Hiddens (communication-specific Hiddens detection and remediation design)
- Framework of Diagnosis (communication failures are often root causes of apparent decision failures)
- Framework of Evidence (evidence quality audit; feedback loop and measurement integrity assessment)
- Framework of Governance (communication governance structure, accountability design, protocol and audit)
- Framework of Decisions (decisions are only as good as the information available; communication architecture shapes option visibility)
- Framework of Interventions (communication redesign as a primary intervention lever)
- Framework of Learning & Adaptation (feedback loops enable learning; communication architecture determines learning capacity)
- Framework of Risk (communication gaps and failures are sources of cascade risk)

### 8c. Targeted Scans (OG §7.5 scans that invoke this framework)

| Targeted Scan | Role of this framework |
|---|---|
| Scan 1: Signal filtering and selective relay | Identify which information is filtered at which boundaries; surface what doesn't reach decision-makers; trigger channel audit and redundancy design. |
| Scan 2: Strategic silence and information withholding | Surface deliberate non-communication; identify who is kept uninformed and why; trigger governance of information access and transparency design. |
| Scan 3: Distortion through relay and cascade | Audit message distortion as it propagates through relay chains; identify most-distorting interfaces; trigger error-control and feedback design. |
| Scan 4: Translation loss and encoding failure | Compare interpretations across users of same encoding; surface what encodings hide; trigger multi-encoding design and annotation. |
| Scan 5: Amplification bias and signal dominance | Identify which signals amplified vs suppressed; audit amplification mechanisms (network effects, incentives); trigger source diversification and amplification audit. |
| Scan 7: Temporal drift and latency in feedback | Audit feedback loop latency; identify drift risk; trigger temporal compression and real-time feedback mechanisms. |
| Scan 9: Authority capture and credibility asymmetry | Surface authority-driven filtering and dismissal of challenge; audit source credibility divergence; trigger protected channels for dissent and feedback. |
