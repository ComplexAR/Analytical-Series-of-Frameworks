## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Culture and Norms framework identifies shared meanings, interpretation rules, norms, and sanctions that shape what agents perceive and how they behave; classifies culture into six meta-categories and thirty core types; profiles culture across five dimensions (Explicitness, Enforcement Strength, Contestation, Stability, Scope); maps five dynamics patterns (Drift, Tipping, Integration Failure, Subculture Divergence, Rapid Change); and surfaces hidden norms, buried tacit rules, and cultural blindspots that enable deviance and dysfunction. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with culture-level visibility failures, enabling downstream frameworks to diagnose behaviour, design interventions, and anticipate cultural resistance or support.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature | Mechanism | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drift & Exception Creep | Standards gradually erode; exceptions become routine; enforcement lapses; "nobody does that anymore." | Incremental violations; weak consequence; normalisation of deviation; path dependence. | Control failures; compliance theatre; hidden rule breakdown until crisis. |
| Tipping Point & Rapid Change | Culture suddenly shifts after a critical incident or leadership change; previously accepted behaviour becomes taboo. | Shock reveals contradiction; new narrative gains dominance; rapid legitimacy shift; feedback loops amplify. | Surprise reversals; residual old-culture actors punished; psychological whiplash. |
| Integration Failure & Culture Clash | Merging entities carry conflicting cultures; boundary stress creates misunderstanding; integration slow or incomplete. | Different norms, meanings, legitimacy rules; poor translation; power asymmetry in assimilation; identity threat. | Coordination failure; dual cultures persist; boundary friction; post-M&A integration problems. |
| Subculture & Counter-Culture | Divergent norms emerge in subgroups; counter-cultures challenge dominant meaning system. | Role/location/cohort-specific norms; identity distinctiveness; challenge to legitimacy; isolation enabling drift. | Fragmentation; hidden practices; internal contestation; dominant culture awareness lag. |
| Rapid Change & Culture Shock | Culture changes faster than people can adapt; meanings shift; old rules become invalid. | External shock (crisis, regulation, new leadership); forced migration; rapid scaling; technology disruption. | Psychological distress; reduced morale; compliance failures; resistance and sabotage. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | Which interpretive defaults are hidden because they are "just how we think"; which categories are invisible? |
| II (Systemic) | Which informal rules and shadow sanctions drive behaviour more than formal policy? |
| III (Informational) | Where are norms about what can be said; who is silent due to retaliation risk? |
| IV (Temporal) | Which cultural drift has occurred; where are old rules still enforced informally? |
| V (Relational) | Where does status suppress truth-telling; whose voices are protected vs exposed? |
| VI (Ontological) | Which meanings are taken-for-granted and unexamined; where are buried metaphors driving action? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Invisible Norms (unwritten rules that are only visible through violation)
- Interpretive Defaults (habitual causal explanations applied reflexively)
- Shadow Rules & Workarounds (real process diverges from policy)
- Norm-Based Silence (retaliation risk prevents truth-telling)
- Status-Protected Behaviour (high-status actors' norm violations forgiven)
- Cultural Blindspots (meaning system prevents seeing certain truths)
- Buried Metaphors (root metaphors shape action but are not examined)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shadow rules driving behaviour more than policy | Framework of Governance; Framework of Evidence | Shadow-process mapping; rules audit; behaviour-policy divergence analysis | Document real rules; update policy to match practice or enforce policy consistently. |
| Status protecting norm violators | Framework of Power; Framework of Responsibility | Norm-violation audit across status levels; consequence analysis; fairness review | Apply norms equally; separate measurement from hierarchy; ensure accountability. |
| Retaliation risk preventing truth-telling | Framework of Communications; Framework of Governance | Safe-channel audit; protected disclosure mechanisms; retaliation cost assessment | Create protected reporting; separate measurement from reward; protect dissent. |
| Cultural drift degrading standards | Framework of Learning; Framework of Governance | Drift timeline; exception creep audit; norm-change tracking | Reinvest in norm enforcement; refresh standards; ritual renewal; leadership modeling. |
| Cultural clash post-integration | Framework of Governance; Framework of Power | Dual-culture mapping; integration friction analysis; legitimacy assessment | Create bridging rituals; translate meanings; manage status anxieties; co-design shared norms. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify focal groups/organisations; observe what is actually done (shadow processes).
2. List key norms (what is required, taboo, celebrated, punished); identify informal enforcement.
3. Identify main interpretive defaults (habitual explanations for failures).
4. Note obvious divergence between stated policy and enacted norms.
5. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan; shortlist top 2–3 hidden norms; flag for Tier 2 if high-consequence.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Map culture across six meta-categories (30 types).
2. Profile each norm/meaning across five dimensions: Explicitness, Enforcement Strength, Contestation, Stability, Scope.
3. Map shadow processes; identify rule-policy divergence.
4. Analyse five dynamic patterns; identify drift, tipping-points, integration friction.
5. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan; identify hidden norms, interpretive defaults, status-protected behaviour.
6. Design cultural interventions (norm reinforcement, meaning translation, ritual renewal, leadership modeling).

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add hidden norms, interpretive defaults, shadow rules with visibility, enforcement, consequence. | Tier 1 scan; escalate high-consequence to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record norm assessments, shadow-process evidence, drift indicators, norm-policy divergence. | After culture profiling; note gaps (e.g., informal enforcement evidence, retaliation risk data). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Hypotheses about hidden norms, drift, integration failure; propose observation/interview tests. | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens. |
| Information Requests | Request policy documents, interview access across levels/cohorts, observation of actual processes, incident records. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection. |

**Gate Question:** Have we mapped culture across six meta-categories, profiled norms across five dimensions, identified shadow processes, analysed five dynamic patterns, run Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and escalated high-consequence hidden norms to Tier 2 or downstream frameworks?
