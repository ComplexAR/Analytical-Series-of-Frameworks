## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Scale framework identifies all relevant scales (geographic, organizational, temporal, population, informational, social/relational) across which a scenario operates; profiles each scale along five dimensions (size/extent, grain/resolution, integration/coupling, heterogeneity/variation, observability); maps scale-dependent dynamics (emergent properties, thresholds, scaling laws, non-linearities); and surfaces scale-related Hiddens (scale mismatch blindness, aggregation artifacts, level confusions). It populates the Hiddens Register and Evidence Ledger with scale completeness checks, enabling detection of scale-level mismatches and invisible dynamics at unanalyzed scales.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emergent Properties at Scale | Properties visible at one scale disappear or reverse at another; aggregation changes character; micro doesn't predict macro. | System behavior nonlinear with respect to scale; emergent properties don't exist in components; phase transitions occur. | Misdiagnosis at wrong scale; interventions designed for one scale fail at another; hidden leverage points. |
| Scale Mismatch Blindness | Problem analyzed at scale A but manifests at scale B; analysis invisible to decision-makers at different scale; coordination fails. | Expertise and incentives are scale-specific; siloes prevent cross-scale learning; attention is local. | Delayed response; uncoordinated action; scale-level strategic failure. |
| Aggregation Artifacts | Averaging or aggregation hides within-scale variation; heterogeneity treated as noise; distributions matter but summarized away. | Convenient aggregation metrics flatten variation; measurement systems designed for summary; diversity de-emphasised. | Decisions wrong for actual population; within-scale variation ignored; tail risks invisible. |
| Non-Linear Scaling | System scales non-linearly; doubling size/scale doesn't double properties; threshold effects are hidden; power laws determine behavior. | Fundamental to system (fractal structure, critical phenomena); not captured by linear extrapolation; phase transitions exist. | Linear projections fail; scale-expansion surprises; intervention dosing wrong. |
| Scale Creep & Boundary Collapse | Effective scale for a process shifts over time; traditional scale boundaries become permeable; system rescales itself. | Technology, communication, or governance enable cross-scale interaction; scale-dependent processes accelerate; boundaries become arbitrary. | Institutions designed for one scale become obsolete; authority/responsibility mismatched; crisis at boundary crossing. |
| Resolution Blindness | Phenomena visible at fine grain/resolution disappear at coarse resolution; variation at grain size is invisible at aggregate. | Data collection, measurement, monitoring operate at coarse scales; fine-grain variation below detection threshold; incentives favor aggregation. | Small-scale variation ignored; bottom-up signals suppressed; surprises when variation becomes visible. |
| Scale Substitution & Lock-in | A scale becomes dominant and alternatives are suppressed; scaling arrangements become locked-in by infrastructure/institutions. | Network effects, economies of scale, or power concentration favor one scale; alternatives become economically inaccessible. | Loss of redundancy; vulnerability to scale-level failure; brittleness. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What scale phenomena are salient vs invisible from common positions; what scale levels dominate attention vs are below notice? |
| II (Systemic) | How do processes at different scales couple and interact; what cross-scale feedback loops and emergent properties are unmapped? |
| III (Informational) | What scales are measured vs unmeasured; what aggregation hides within-scale variation; what measurement artifacts distort scale profiles? |
| IV (Temporal) | How do timescales vary across spatial/organizational scales; what slow-moving scale-level dynamics are invisible on short horizons? |
| V (Relational) | Whose scale is dominant in framing; whose scale-level interests are suppressed; what power asymmetries hide scale-level alternatives? |
| VI (Ontological) | What scales are not yet conceived as relevant; what emergent scale phenomena are unnameable in current ontologies? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Scale Blindness (certain scales invisible from dominant positions)
- Aggregation Artifacts (within-scale variation hidden by averaging)
- Non-Linear Surprise (scaling laws or threshold effects unmapped)
- Scale Mismatch (problem and solution at different scales; action incoherent)
- Resolution Loss (fine-grain variation lost at coarse measurement)
- Scale Lock-in (alternative scales become economically/structurally inaccessible)
- Emergent Suppression (scale-level properties not yet integrated into models)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aggregation hiding variation | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Metrics | Distributional analysis; fine-grain data; heterogeneity audit | Disaggregate data; map within-scale variation; tail-risk analysis. |
| Scale mismatch or incoherent action | Framework of Systems; Framework of Decisions | Multi-scale coordination map; scale-alignment audit | Identify scale of problem, solution, authority; align levers. |
| Non-linear threshold or tipping point | Framework of Causation; Framework of Risk | Threshold analysis; phase-transition modelling; bifurcation testing | Model non-linearities; test threshold proximity; design scale-contingent interventions. |
| Cross-scale feedback loop | Framework of Systems; Framework of Hiddens | Multi-scale feedback mapping; reflexive effect analysis | Map upscaling/downscaling loops; test reflexive stability. |
| Scale lock-in or path dependency | Framework of Interventions; Framework of Learning | Alternative scale design; scale transition pathways | Envision rescaling; design transition; manage switching costs. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify focal scales: list relevant geographic, organizational, temporal, population, informational, and social scales present in scenario.
2. For each focal scale, assess two key properties: Size/Extent (how large) and Integration/Coupling (how tightly coupled to other phenomena).
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six meta-categories; flag obvious scale blindness (which scales are below attention threshold) and aggregation artifacts.
4. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence scale mismatches or scale-level phenomena; note if non-linear scaling or emergent properties are suspected; flag for escalation.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Scale inventory: list all focal scales across six scale types (I–VI); for each, record extent/size, grain/resolution, coupling, heterogeneity, observability.
2. Dimensional profiles by scale: for each focal scale, identify salient dimensions and how dimensional values vary across the scale; note scaling laws if known.
3. Cross-scale coupling map: sketch how scales interact (upscaling/downscaling pathways); identify feedback loops, emergent properties, tipping points; note non-linearities.
4. Aggregation audit: identify metrics and measurements; assess whether aggregation artifacts hide within-scale variation; evaluate whether aggregation levels are appropriate for decision needs.
5. Scale-dependent dynamics: analyze which dynamics (thresholds, phases, heterogeneity effects) manifest at which scales; identify scale-level specific risks.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence scale-level phenomenon, identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; propose scale-sensitivity tests.
7. Limits register: document unmeasurable scales; record scale-level uncertainty; flag reflexivity (analysis itself operates at a scale that shapes observability).

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add scale-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag (Perceptual, Systemic, Informational, Temporal, Relational, Ontological); mechanism; consequence. | After Tier 1 scan; escalate scale mismatches and non-linear surprises. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record scale-specific observations, measurements, scaling laws, aggregation effects; note data gaps at neglected scales. | After scale inventory and dimensional profiling; flag unmeasured scales. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about scale-dependent phenomena, non-linearities, emergent properties; propose scale-sensitivity tests. | Identified Unknowns and scale mismatches; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request fine-grain data at neglected scales; request scaling laws and thresholds; request alternative scale representations. | To reduce scale Unknowns; coordinate with Evidence and Causation frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified all focal scales (I–VI), profiled each scale's properties, mapped cross-scale coupling and emergent phenomena, audited aggregation effects, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and identified scale-appropriate interventions?
