## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Incentives framework identifies what material, social, normative, and temporal consequences reward or punish agent choices; classifies incentives across six meta-categories and thirty core types; profiles each incentive across five dimensions (Source, Valence, Alignment, Scope, Transparency); maps twelve dynamic patterns (Crowding Out, Gaming, Drift, Cascade, Adaptation); and surfaces hidden, distorted, or denied incentives that produce moral hazard, principal–agent failure, and gaming. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with incentive-level blindnesses and redesign requirements, enabling downstream frameworks to align structures, governance, and interventions with true incentive exposure rather than declared intent.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crowding Out | External incentive undermines intrinsic motivation; shift from "I want to" to "only if paid" | Transactional framing replaces intrinsic framing; autonomy perception decreases. | Erodes ethical commitment; increases control costs and rigidity when incentive removed. |
| Feedback & Habit | Repeated reward creates habit and expectation; effects compound or diminish with repetition. | Reinforcement loop locks in behaviour; expectations become inelastic. | Locks in behaviour (positive) or brittle habits (negative) if reward removed. |
| Threshold & Tipping | Incentive has no effect until threshold crossed, then sharp shift; or small change tips system to new state. | Nonlinear response to incentive magnitude; critical thresholds exist. | Sudden unexpected shifts; cascade failures; risk of system flip. |
| Drift & Goal Displacement | Incentive structure persists while underlying goal changes; agents continue optimizing to obsolete target. | Inertia in incentive design; lag between goal change and incentive update. | False confidence; performance looks good on old metrics while outcomes fail. |
| Path Dependence & Lock-In | Early incentive choices constrain later options; costly to change even when misaligned. | Sunk costs, entrenched expectations, political difficulty of change. | Prevents adaptation; locks in perverse incentives; change becomes expensive. |
| Incentive Cascade | Change at one level triggers unintended changes at another level (CEO bonus affects frontline gaming). | Interdependence across levels; incentive propagation through hierarchy. | Can amplify or invert intended effects; creates coupling and hidden risks. |
| Regime Shift | Gradual changes reach tipping point and system behaviour shifts into new regime. | Accumulation of small changes; feedback loops reinforce shift. | Shifts surprise decision-makers; recovery from some regimes is difficult. |
| Adaptation & Workaround | Agents learn to achieve payoff while subverting objectives; 'shadow economy' emerges. | Learning and creative circumvention; gaps between official and real objectives. | True outcomes diverge from reported; efficiency loss to workarounds. |
| Equity Perception | Agents perceive incentive change as unfair even if objectively sensible; moral outrage persists. | Reference point effects; fairness norms; identity threat. | Triggers exit, sabotage, or norm violation despite rational appeal. |
| Reference Point Instability | Same incentive feels different at different status levels; non-linear effects. | Agents evaluate relative to reference points, not absolute levels. | Identical incentive produces opposite behavioural response by cohort. |
| Attention Capture | High-salience incentive distorts priority-setting; low-visibility risks neglected. | Salience gradients distort attention allocation. | Can produce system failure in low-salience domains. |
| Substitution & Migration | Agent substitutes one incentive source for another; effort migrates to higher-yield domain. | Multiple incentive sources available; agents allocate effort to maximize yield. | Renders intended incentive ineffective if substitutes available. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | Which incentives operate as background conditions that analysts overlook; which stated motivations mask rational responses to true incentives? |
| II (Systemic) | Which incentives are embedded in KPIs and accountability mechanics that systematically bias behaviour; where is institutional structure itself perverse? |
| III (Informational) | Which incentives and performance data are filtered, distorted, or withheld; where does official reporting diverge from reality? |
| IV (Temporal) | Which incentives have drifted into misalignment over time; what legacy arrangements persist despite changed context? |
| V (Relational) | Which incentives are socially unsafe to admit; where do status and power determine which incentives can be voiced? |
| VI (Ontological) | Which incentives lacks categories to represent (beyond money/compliance); which moral and identity incentives are invisible? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Principal–Agent Misalignment (divergent payoffs, risk exposures, time horizons)
- Moral Hazard & Risk Externalisation (insulation from downside encourages excessive risk)
- Perverse Metric Optimisation (Goodhart's Law; metrics diverge from purpose)
- Single-Dimension Focus (financial only; ignoring social, identity, temporal incentives)
- Static Assumption (treating incentives as fixed rather than dynamic)
- Gaming & Workaround Economies (shadow incentives and unmeasured practices)
- Incentive Cascade & Coupling (unintended consequences across levels)
- Positional Invisibility (different roles experience same incentive differently)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unexpected non-compliance or gaming | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Causation | Behaviour-first mapping; compare stated vs revealed preferences | Audit actual incentives; test "what must be true for this to be rational?" |
| Metric divergence from outcomes | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Metrics | Goodhart audit; unintended consequence scan | Combine quantitative and qualitative indicators; test for gaming vulnerabilities. |
| Principal–agent failure or misalignment | Framework of Governance; Framework of Responsibility | Principal–agent test; incentive audit | Compare principal's stated goals to agent's observed behaviour; align payoffs and oversight. |
| Cascade across organisational levels | Framework of Systems; Framework of Causation | Incentive cascade mapping; stress-test scenario | Map incentive interfaces across levels; separate incentives where appropriate. |
| Pervasive compliance theatre | Framework of Culture; Framework of Narratives | Narrative–incentive audit; stress test with independent evidence | Look for divergence between narrative and lived reality; rebalance incentive portfolio. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. List focal agents and decision points; identify observable actions and outcomes.
2. For each agent/decision, map visible rewards, penalties, and expectations (formal and informal); note obvious gaps.
3. Scan across three highest-relevance meta-categories (typically Material, Normative, Structural); note any wholly missing categories.
4. Identify obvious misalignments (incentive conflicts, gaming opportunities, perverse incentives).
5. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan across six meta-categories; shortlist top 2–3 hidden incentives or gaming risks; flag for Tier 2 if high-consequence.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Map all focal agents, roles, and decision points; identify actions and outcomes for analysis.
2. Systematically scan across six meta-categories (I–VI) for each agent/role; populate complete incentive inventory with provenance.
3. For each salient incentive (top 5–10), profile across five dimensions: Source (Intrinsic/Extrinsic), Valence & Mode (Positive/Negative, Simple/Complex), Alignment (Aligned/Perverse), Scope & Level (Dyadic/Ecosystem), Transparency (Explicit/Hidden).
4. Analyse all twelve dynamic patterns; identify which are active (nascent, present, high-risk); trace cascades, feedback loops, path dependence.
5. Assess five interface types: Contractual & Principal–Agent (alignment, monitoring, controls); Regulatory & Compliance (capture, loopholes); Platform & Algorithmic (feedback loops, gaming); Relational & Reputation (suppression, positional asymmetry); Mission & Narrative (identity/meaning fit).
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-relevance Hidden (Distortion, Mirage, Framing, Secret, Fragmentation, Positional, Ghost/Shadow, Contextual), identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; assign detection and remediation interfaces.
7. Design incentive redesign options; document residual Unknowns about hidden incentives, gaming risks, or adaptation pathways; apply Escalation Rule; generate closure artefacts and monitoring plan.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add shortlist of incentive-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag (I–VI), mechanism, detectability, consequence, strategic/accidental. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record incentive classifications, dimensional assessments, dynamic pattern presence/absence, evidence quality, triangulation sources, gaps. | After incentive profiling and validation; note gaps (e.g., informal incentive evidence, gaming indicators, reference point testing). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about hidden incentives, gaming vulnerabilities, misalignment persistence, principal–agent conflicts; propose tests (behaviour audits, provenance checks, stress tests). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request incentive policy documentation, KPI/metric definitions, compensation data, audit reports, behaviour data, interview access across levels. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Evidence and Governance frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we mapped all focal agents' incentives across six meta-categories, profiled salient incentives across five dimensions, analysed twelve dynamic patterns, assessed five interface types, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and escalated any high-consequence hidden/misaligned incentives to Tier 2/3 or downstream frameworks (Governance, Intervention, Risk)?
