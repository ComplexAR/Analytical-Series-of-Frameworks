## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Governance framework classifies governance mechanisms, decision authority, and accountability structures through six meta-categories and thirty core types, profiles governance across five dimensions (transparency, participation, authority distribution, accountability clarity, adaptability), maps ten dynamics of how governance succeeds or fails, and surfaces governance-specific visibility failures via tiered Hiddens scans. It enables governance design aligned to decision stakes and reversibility, assessment of authority distribution and accountability clarity, transparency and dissent-protection design, and adaptive governance structures that enable course correction and learning. It supports both individual-accountability models (decision-maker responsibility) and collective-responsibility models (shared governance).

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What authority relationships and power dynamics are unspoken or unacknowledged; what informal authority operates differently than formal? |
| II (Systemic) | How do institutional structures and procedures embed particular accountability patterns; what structural accountability gaps exist? |
| III (Informational) | What information about decisions, outcomes, or governance processes is restricted, gated, or selectively disclosed? |
| IV (Temporal) | What historical accountability decisions or governance patterns shape current structures; what precedent constraints exist? |
| V (Relational) | What dissent is suppressed due to retaliation risk or psychological safety concerns; what minority voice is marginalised? |
| VI (Ontological) | What governance paradigm forecloses alternative governance approaches; what is treated as the only way to govern? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms

- Authority and power asymmetry (informal authority differences from formal; power unacknowledged)
- Accountability diffusion (responsibility scattered; nobody accountable)
- Transparency erosion (information gating; disclosure restricted)
- Dissent suppression and conformity enforcement (minority voice marginalised; retaliation risk)
- Governance capture (formal process subverted; power influence covert)
- Reversibility authority loss (decisions become irreversible; adaptation constrained)
- Escalation threshold drift (oversight frequency/intensity reduced; surprise risk)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Authority-accountability mismatch | Framework of Governance (redesign); Framework of Responsibility | Authority audit; accountability mapping; decision-outcome consequence analysis | Realign authority with accountability; clarify decision rights and responsibility |
| Dissent suppressed; retaliation risk | Framework of Culture & Norms; Framework of Governance | Dissent-safety assessment; anonymous feedback; protective mechanism design | Strengthen psychological safety; protect dissent; remove retaliation risks; amplify minority voice |
| Transparency erosion; information gating | Framework of Governance (transparency redesign); Framework of Hiddens | Access audit; gating justification review; transparency requirement design | Widen disclosure; justify restriction; design transparent decision-making; increase access |
| Reversibility authority declining | Framework of Governance (structure redesign) | Reversal-authority audit; path-dependence analysis; adaptive-governance design | Preserve reversal authority; reduce sunk-cost anchoring; design for course correction |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Map formal authority structure: who decides what; how is authority distributed; what reversibility authority exists.
2. Assess accountability clarity: who is accountable for each decision; what are consequences; does authority match accountability?
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: check for authority concentration, accountability diffusion, dissent suppression, transparency erosion, governance capture.
4. Identify 2–3 highest-risk governance vulnerabilities (single-point-of-failure, diffused accountability, captured process).
5. Test psychological safety: do people feel safe raising concerns; is dissent protected or punished?

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Map formal and informal authority structures; identify gaps and misalignments between formal authority and actual power.
2. Classify governance types (meta-categories I–VI); profile across five dimensions (transparency, participation, authority distribution, accountability clarity, adaptability).
3. Audit accountability: who is held accountable for what decisions; what are consequences; do authority and accountability align?
4. Assess transparency: what information is openly accessible; what is restricted and why; are restrictions justified and appropriately bounded?
5. Evaluate dissent handling: how are disagreements addressed; what is the retaliation risk; is minority voice amplified or suppressed?
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence Hidden (authority capture, accountability diffusion, dissent suppression, transparency erosion), design detection and remediation interfaces.
7. Produce governance assessment: identify strengths and vulnerabilities; recommend redesign priorities for high-risk areas.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Governance Documentation | Clarify authority, accountability, transparency, dissent-protection, escalation structures; document decision rights and reversibility authority | Pre-closure documentation |
| Information Requests | Request access to decision records; request outcome monitoring data; request dissent-safety assessment | To support governance audit |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we mapped formal and informal authority, assessed accountability-authority alignment, tested dissent safety, run Tier 1 Hiddens scan for authority capture and accountability diffusion, and identified governance redesign priorities for high-risk vulnerabilities?
