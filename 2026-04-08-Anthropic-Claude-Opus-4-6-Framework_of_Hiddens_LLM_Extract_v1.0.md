## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Hiddens framework identifies, classifies, and remediates all visibility failures—information, phenomena, patterns, risks, and mechanisms that are suppressed, distorted, or rendered invisible to decision-makers through six meta-categories and thirty core Hidden types. Simultaneously, it operates as the visibility audit layer: all other frameworks generate Hiddens input, and Hiddens cross-checks validate that no critical reality-relevant factor has been excluded from analysis. It profiles Hiddens across five dimensions (mechanism, detectability, agency, consequence, reducibility), maps ten dynamics of how Hiddens form and persist, and generates tiered remediation guidance (Tier 1 scan, Tier 2 deepening, Tier 3 escalation) with specific detection and remediation interfaces for each Hidden type.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature | Mechanism | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hidden formation through routine | Conscious choice becomes standard procedure becomes invisible default; visibility erodes through habituation | Efficiency pressure; cost of conscious choice; institutional inertia; no active maintenance of alternative | Hidden persists unchallenged; becomes treated as "how things are" rather than contingent choice |
| Detectability erosion | Initially visible Hidden becomes progressively harder to see; defenders develop rationalisation and normalisation; monitoring atrophies | Cognitive adaptation; institutional routines embed invisibility; incentive to minimise detection; active suppression | Hidden deeply entrenched; requires external shock or deliberate unmasking to surface |
| Remediation resistance | Attempts to surface or remedy Hidden trigger defensive responses; remediation threatened as illegitimate challenge; suppression hardens | Identity stakes; power threat; investment in current arrangements; organisational commitment to status quo | Remediation blocked; escalation to Tier 3 required; external intervention or crisis needed |
| Hidden-Independent interaction | Multiple Hiddens reinforce each other; addressing one without others leaves protection intact; compound concealment emerges | Interdependence of gating, distortion, and paradigm boundaries; multiple mechanisms protecting same reality | Partial remediation ineffective; systemic Hidden-removal required; single-mechanism intervention insufficient |
| Visibility shock | Hidden suddenly surfaces through unexpected observation, external challenge, or failure; surprise and disruption follow | Internal awareness increases; external exposure forces recognition; failure reveals prior invisibility; whistleblower disclosure | Opportunity for remediation but also organisational trauma; sensemaking and accountability confusion; defensive responses likely |
| Remediation reversion | Hidden is surfaced, remediation attempted, but Hiddens recur; gain is temporary; protective mechanisms reconstitute | Underlying cause not addressed; defensive motivation remains; incentive structure unchanged; root mechanism persists | Remediation fatigue; cynicism about change; cyclical Hidden reform; persistent susceptibility |
| Hidden legitimation | Once a Hidden is established, it gains institutional legitimacy; norm becomes sacred; alternative becomes unthinkable | Organisational inertia; sunk costs; cultural integration; legal/regulatory embedding | Even explicit challenge produces strong defensive response; requires significant event or authority shift to overcome |
| Agency distribution in Hiddens | Responsibility for Hidden distributed across many actors, each with bounded responsibility; collective responsibility evaporates; no explicit maintainer | Diffused responsibility; no single actor clearly accountable; each plays small role; systematic effect emerges from aggregate | Accountability for remedy diffuses; each actor has incentive to shift responsibility; change requires coordination impossible absent authority |
| Cross-framework Hiddens coherence | Hiddens exist in multiple frameworks simultaneously; revealing Hidden in one framework requires coordination with others; isolation blocks full remediation | Hidden protection mechanisms scattered across frameworks; Beliefs, Culture & Norms, Power, Governance all protect same Hidden | Coordinated multi-framework remediation required; single-framework work insufficient; integration work essential |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What is observable but not being perceived; what percept al defaults or filters exclude; what does bounded attention miss? |
| II (Systemic) | What institutional structures, procedures, incentives embed invisibility; what is "how the system works" but never named or questioned? |
| III (Informational) | What information is gated, classified, or selectively disclosed; what data transformations hide provenance; what measurement systems are opaque? |
| IV (Temporal) | What is hidden by time-horizon choices, historical distance, or trend lags; what would be visible over longer timescale? |
| V (Relational) | Who knows what information; what power asymmetries determine access; what retaliation risks silence truth-telling; what dissent is suppressed? |
| VI (Ontological) | What paradigm boundaries make certain realities unthinkable; what assumptions foreclose alternatives; what is treated as fact but reflects choice? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms

- Perceptual filtering and inattentional blindness (what is unnoticed)
- Structural embedding and institutional routines (what is invisible as structure)
- Information gating and selective disclosure (what is withheld or restricted)
- Temporal framing and historical distance (what is hidden by time perspective)
- Power-driven suppression and silencing (what is actively hidden due to power)
- Paradigm boundaries and ontological closure (what is outside frame of thinkability)
- Normalisation of deviance and assumption invisibility (what is treated as inevitable)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| Hidden type | Detection interface | Remediation interface | Escalation condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Perceptual Hiddens | Direct observation; attention experiment; schema-challenge | Attention redirection; training; cross-training to break filters | Entrenched in identity or institutional routine |
| Systemic Hiddens | Structural audit; procedure listing; stakeholder mapping | Structural redesign; procedure change; role redesign; incentive redesign | Sunk costs and path dependency strong; requires authority shift |
| Informational Hiddens | Access audit; gating documentation; information request tracking | Widen access; increase transparency; redesign information systems | Legal/regulatory restriction or security requirement present |
| Temporal Hiddens | Timeline analysis; historical comparison; longer-horizon scenario | Expand time horizons; increase monitoring frequency; reduce discount rates | Distributed temporal understanding makes coordination difficult |
| Relational Hiddens | Anonymous feedback; power audit; retaliation-risk assessment; dissent mapping | Equalize information access; strengthen psychological safety; protect dissent channels | Power asymmetry structural; authority alignment required |
| Ontological Hiddens | Paradigm exposure; alternative-frame testing; boundary testing | Paradigm-challenge sessions; perspective-taking; option expansion | Paradigm defended by identity/sacred values; requires identity work |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Run Tier 1 scan across all six Hiddens meta-categories; for each, ask whether Hiddens of that type are likely active in this situation.
2. Identify 3–5 candidate Hiddens with highest consequence; rate mechanism (how is it maintained?), detectability (how observable?), consequence (what is at stake?).
3. For each candidate, suggest one detection interface (how to surface it) and one remediation interface (how to address it).
4. Flag any Hiddens that span multiple frameworks (e.g., Hiddens about beliefs, power, and culture together); mark for escalation if multi-framework coordination required.
5. Record Hiddens Register findings (candidate Hiddens with mechanism, consequence, interfaces).

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Run Tier 1 scan across all six meta-categories systematically; for each candidate Hidden, assess mechanism, detectability, agency, consequence, reducibility.
2. Develop Tier 2 working models for top 5–8 Hiddens: for each, specify mechanism (how is it produced and maintained?), detection interface (specific tests/probes to surface), remediation interface (specific interventions).
3. Analyse Hidden interactions: how do Hiddens reinforce each other; what compound concealments exist; what multi-framework coordination is needed?
4. Map dynamics: trace how Hiddens form, entrench, resist remediation; identify detectability erosion and remediation reversion risks.
5. Cross-check all other frameworks (Beliefs, Evidence, Failures, etc.) against Hiddens Register: have all identified Hiddens across frameworks been captured; are there blind spots in Hiddens detection?
6. If indicated, invoke Targeted Hiddens Discovery Scans from OG §7.5 (Belief Persistence, Positional Blindness, Paradigm Boundary, Silent Knowledge, Normalisation of Deviance, etc.).
7. Produce remediation roadmap with prioritisation (high-consequence low-detectability Hiddens require first attention); design remediation sequencing and escalation triggers.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Populate with all identified Hiddens (candidate and confirmed); meta-category; mechanism; detectability; consequence; detection interface; remediation interface | After Tier 1 and Tier 2 scans |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Propose detection and remediation tests for top Hiddens; prioritise by consequence and detectability | For all high-consequence Hiddens |
| Closure Artefacts | Residual Unknowns (Hiddens that remain opaque), acceptability rationale (why unresolved Hiddens are acceptable), monitoring plan (how to detect if Hidden deepens) | Pre-closure validation |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we run Tier 1 scan across all six meta-categories, identified top candidate Hiddens, assessed mechanism and consequence for each, designed detection and remediation interfaces, checked for multi-framework Hidden interactions, and escalated high-consequence Hiddens to appropriate remediation frameworks?
