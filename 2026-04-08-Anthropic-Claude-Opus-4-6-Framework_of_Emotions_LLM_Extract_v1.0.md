## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Framework of Emotions identifies what affective states—fear, anger, grief, pride, shame, solidarity, despair, awe, and others—are driving behaviour, shaping perception, and structuring social dynamics in a scenario. It classifies emotions into six meta-categories (thirty core types spanning primary emotions, secondary social emotions, self-evaluative emotions, moral emotions, and existential emotions); maps their intensity, visibility, action readiness, and suppression mechanisms; surfaces emotional suppression norms and the hiding mechanisms (rationalisation, denial, collective invisibility) that render emotions opaque to decision-makers; and provides detection and remediation interfaces for surfacing hidden emotional drivers. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with emotion-specific blindnesses and action pathways, enabling downstream frameworks to design interventions that work with rather than against emotional dynamics.

|---|---|---|---|

| Emotional Contagion & Cascade Effects | Emotions spread rapidly through groups without explicit communication; collective emotional responses appear to emerge spontaneously; synchronised affective states | Humans mirror affects; emotional intensity is contagious; group identity amplifies emotional resonance; one actor's fear or anger activates similar states in others | Small emotional triggers produce disproportionate group responses; emotional escalation accelerates through networks; collective emotions override individual restraint |
| Emotional Intensification Through Framing | Same situation generates different emotional intensity depending on how it is framed (threat vs opportunity, moral vs practical, collective vs individual) | Threat-framing activates fear and anger; moral framing activates outrage; collective framing activates solidarity; each frame amplifies different emotional responses | Emotional intensity can be manipulated through language and framing; groups respond differently to identical situations depending on narrative frame; emotional escalation may be strategic |
| Emotional Labour & Compliance | Surface emotional display does not match internal emotional state; actors perform feelings to meet organisational/cultural expectations while experiencing different emotions privately | Norms require emotional conformity (enthusiasm, positivity, loyalty); non-conformity is punished; emotional display becomes work ("emotional labour"); real emotions become invisible | Compliance appears genuine until it fails (exit, sabotage, burnout); teams appear engaged while being emotionally alienated; interventions fail because emotional alienation is invisible |
| Emotional Escalation & Lock-in | Emotional intensity increases through cycles of trigger, response, counter-trigger; as intensity increases, emotional reasoning takes over from deliberative reasoning; actors become locked into emotional positions | Early emotional trigger (fear, anger) initiates response; response triggers opponent; emotional intensity amplifies; rational deliberation gives way to affective reaction; emotional commitment makes retreat costly | De-escalation becomes increasingly difficult as emotional intensity increases; actors appear irrational because affective reasoning dominates; crisis response may be driven by emotional lock-in rather than circumstance |
| Emotional Memory & Trauma Activation | Past emotional events shape current emotional responses; trauma or deep grievance can activate suddenly when circumstances resemble prior situation | Emotional memory is stronger than factual memory; anniversary effects and trigger reactivation occur; similar situations activate disproportionate responses; deep emotions persist despite time | Emotional response appears disproportionate to current situation until prior emotional history is understood; cycles of conflict recur on emotional rather than rational drivers |
| Emotional Suppression Cascade | Suppression of one emotion cascades into suppression or amplification of others; systemic emotional suppression produces delayed explosive release | Suppressed grief (denial of necessary losses) blocks processing and locks in frozen affect; suppressed anger becomes passive-aggressive or explosive rage; suppressed fear becomes recklessness | Emotional systems become unstable; suppressed emotions re-emerge in unexpected forms or contexts; cascade of suppression makes emotional landscape unpredictable and fragile |
| Collective Denial & Emotional Coherence Seeking | Groups suppress uncomfortable emotions to maintain false coherence; shared denial maintains group identity; acknowledging suppressed emotions threatens group unity | Group norms enforce emotional conformity; admitting divergent emotions threatens inclusion; groups collectively deny realities that would require emotional reckoning | Crisis response fails because group has suppressed emotions necessary for adaptive response; teams cannot grieve necessary losses; collective denial makes adaptation impossible |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What emotional states are invisible to decision-makers because they fall outside typical professional attention or are not expected to be present in this context? |
| II (Systemic) | How are emotional suppression norms embedded in organisational culture as control mechanisms, making certain emotions literally unspeakable or dangerous to express? |
| III (Informational) | What emotional undercurrents contradict the stated information or decision rationale; what affective signals (tone, body language, silence) contradict verbal content? |
| IV (Temporal) | How are emotional memory and past affective events shaping current responses; what prior traumas or losses are being reactivated by current circumstances? |
| V (Relational) | What emotions are flowing between people through contagion; what collective emotional states are emerging that override individual emotional preferences or restraint? |
| VI (Ontological) | What concepts of emotion are operative in this context; what forms of emotional experience are even possible given how emotions are understood (as rational, shameful, unmanageable, natural)? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Emotional Suppression & Rationalisation (affective drivers explained away with rational justifications; behaviour attributed to logic when driven by emotion)
- Emotional Invisibility & Norms (cultural or organisational norms making certain emotions unspeakable or dangerous; emotional conformity enforced; authentic emotions hidden)
- Emotional Contagion Invisibility (collective emotional states emerging through networks that override individual preference; group emotions treated as individual choice)
- Emotional Labour & Performance (surface emotional display masking internal emotional state; compliance appearing genuine while emotional alienation is hidden)
- Emotional Suppression Cascades (suppression of one emotion triggering cascade into other emotional states; emotional system instability hidden behind surface)
- Affective-Cognitive Misalignment (statements about beliefs contradict emotional engagement; actors say one thing emotionally enact different priorities)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Behaviour contradicts stated rational position; stated incentives don't explain observed choices | Interests framework; Decisions framework | Behaviour-first analysis; affective signal attention (tone, body language, silence) | Acknowledge emotional drivers underlying resistance; ask "What are you feeling about this decision?"; make emotion visible as data not noise |
| Emotional suppression norms preventing authentic discussion; false consensus masking disagreement | Culture framework; Conflict framework | Psychological safety assessment; norm re-negotiation; safe channels for authentic expression | Create contexts where authentic emotions can surface; validate different emotional experiences; acknowledge that suppression creates compliance without commitment |
| Emotional contagion driving collective response; small triggers producing disproportionate group reactions | Relations framework; Communications framework | Network analysis for emotional transmission; timeline of emotional escalation; trigger identification | Interrupt contagion cycles; make collective emotional dynamics visible; address triggers rather than attempting to suppress emotional response |
| Teams appear compliant but are emotionally alienated; surface engagement masks disconnection | Culture framework; Learning framework | Exit/retention analysis; private interviews about emotional experience; gap analysis (stated engagement vs private feeling) | Acknowledge gap between surface compliance and emotional reality; create space for emotional authenticity; redesign work/culture to reduce emotional labour burden |
| Emotional escalation locked in; de-escalation appears impossible; crisis appears inevitable | Conflict framework; Governance framework | Emotional intensity tracking; commitment tactic identification; face-saving alternative design | Provide paths for emotional cooling; offer face-saving exits from commitment positions; address underlying emotional drivers (fear, humiliation, moral outrage) |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify the focal actors and the situation triggering emotional engagement; note explicit emotional language and emotional suppression signals (silence, denial, rationalisation where emotions should be present).
2. Map primary emotions lightly across six meta-categories: identify obviously present emotions (fear, anger, grief, pride, shame, hope); note emotional suppression or denial where emotions appear to be missing.
3. Assess emotional visibility: for each emotion identified or inferred, assess whether it is openly acknowledged, performed/suppressed, or completely hidden; note suppression norms preventing expression.
4. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six Hiddens categories lightly; flag obvious emotional invisibility (norms making emotions unspeakable, contagion spreading emotional states, suppression cascades).
5. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence hidden emotions; note whether they are organisationally-suppressed (norms), strategically-concealed (intentional hiding), or latent (unconsciously pursued); flag for escalation to Full Depth if suppression is strong or consequences are high.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Map affective climate across all focal actors: conduct affective inventory for each actor across all thirty emotion types; assess consciousness (explicit, inferred from behaviour, completely latent); assess visibility (openly stated, performed, suppressed, hidden).
2. Assess five cross-cutting dimensions for each emotion: Intensity (mild to overwhelming), Duration (momentary to chronic), Focal clarity (clear trigger vs diffuse), Social visibility (openly expressed vs hidden), Action readiness (what behaviour does it motivate).
3. Analyse emotional dynamics: for each identified emotion, assess which dynamic patterns are active (suppression, contagion, escalation, labour, cascade, alignment); identify cascades and feedback loops.
4. Map suppression mechanisms: identify cultural/organisational norms, hierarchy constraints, fear-based compliance, and strategic concealment that keeps emotions hidden; assess mechanisms' strength and what would disrupt suppression.
5. Analyse emotional contagion pathways: map how emotions spread through networks; identify nodes of contagion; assess which emotions are most contagious and why; design interventions addressing contagion rather than fighting it.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each identified Hidden (e.g., emotional suppression, affective-cognitive misalignment, contagion), assess mechanism, detectability, consequence; assign detection and remediation interfaces; design specific probes and engagement approaches.
7. Map emotional-behavioural implications: understand how hidden emotions drive behaviour (compliance vs resistance, engagement vs alienation, escalation vs de-escalation); generate redesign options acknowledging emotions rather than suppressing them.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add shortlist of emotion-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag (I–VI), emotion name(s), suppression mechanism (norm-based, strategic, cascade), detectability, consequence, remediation interface pointer. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden (e.g., collective trauma, emotional lock-in, suppression cascade) to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record emotional identification, intensity/visibility assessments, suppression mechanisms, contagion pathways, evidence quality, gaps (e.g., what would confirm latent emotion; what affective signals reveal hidden emotion). | After emotional mapping and dimensional profiling; note gaps requiring additional evidence collection (interviews, observation, affective signal attention). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about emotion-driven behaviour, suppression cascade effects, contagion pathways, and emotional escalation trajectories; propose tests (behaviour-emotion alignment analysis, affective signal attention, contagion pathway mapping). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request access to affective climate data (cultural/organisational emotional norms), communications archives (tone and valence), behavioural data revealing emotional drivers, interviews with emotional authenticity permission. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Culture, Conflict, and Communication frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified the focal actors, mapped primary emotions across all six meta-categories, assessed emotional visibility and suppression mechanisms, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, identified highest-consequence hidden emotions (suppression, contagion, cascade effects), and escalated emotional lock-in or collective trauma to appropriate frameworks (Conflict, Governance)?
