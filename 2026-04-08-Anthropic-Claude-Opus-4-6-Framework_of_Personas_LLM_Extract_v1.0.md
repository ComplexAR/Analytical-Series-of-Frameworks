## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Personas framework profiles how agents adopt context-dependent patterns of stance, role-performance, and engagement across eight meta-categories (Epistemic, Dispositional, Motivational, Relational, Cognitive, Temporal, Functional, Hierarchical); characterises each persona across five dimensions (Authenticity, Stability, Visibility, Scope, Intensity); maps dynamics patterns (Shifting, Conflict, Interaction, Evolution, Revelation); and surfaces hidden personas, theatre, and positional invisibility that prevent accurate engagement design. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with persona-level visibility failures and intervention recommendations, enabling downstream frameworks to design engagement, governance, and safeguarding aligned with true motivation rather than performance.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Persona Shifting | Agent changes personas with context (stakes, incentives, audience) creating inconsistency across episodes. | Triggers (situational, relational, incentive, temporal) activate different personas from available set. | Prediction errors and miscalibrated engagement; apparent hypocrisy may be strategic context-switching. |
| Persona Conflict | Mixed signals, hesitation, oscillation when incompatible personas simultaneously active (Steward vs Opportunist). | Internal tensions or role conflicts create behaviour instability; agent caught between competing demands. | Stalled decisions, unreliable commitments, blurred accountability; conflict can be leveraged or mitigated. |
| Persona Interaction | Predictable dyadic/triadic dynamics when specific personas meet (Champion vs Blocker, Leader vs Leader). | Complementarity, antagonism, and power asymmetry shape exchanges; one persona may convert or suppress another. | Outcomes depend on persona composition, not just individual intent; composition predicts failure/success. |
| Persona Evolution | Slow drift in persona over time (Supporter→Champion; Reformer→Traditionalist) as experience accumulates. | Learning, habituation, reward history, and identity reinforcement re-shape default personas. | Long-term trajectories shift coalition capability; early signals predict later resistance or support. |
| Persona Revelation | Hidden, strategic, or self-deceived personas become visible under stress or when incentives change. | Contradictions accumulate until exposed; secrets leak; behaviour under pressure reveals underlying orientation. | Late discovery of sabotage/malevolence drives severe harm; sudden trust collapse and governance crises. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VIII)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | Which personas are inferred from sparse evidence; which salient behaviours mask true orientation; which confidence ratings are overconfident? |
| II (Systemic) | Which personas are stabilised by institutional narratives or scapegoating regardless of evidence; which role lock-ins exist? |
| III (Informational) | Which personas are deliberately concealed; where is key evidence withheld or fragmented; where do private and public performances diverge? |
| IV (Temporal) | Which personas shift under stress or evolve over time; where are delays in detecting persona evolution or sudden transitions? |
| V (Relational) | Where does authority suppress truth or limit what can be said; which personas are invisible from certain positions; where is positional blindness active? |
| VI (Ontological) | Which personas are miscategorised or do not fit standard eight categories; where are category gaps or new hybrid personas emerging? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Persona Essentialism (treating personas as fixed traits rather than context-dependent modes)
- Attribution Error (explaining behaviour solely by persona while ignoring constraints and incentives)
- Theatre Capture (accepting declared or visible personas without behavioural verification; over-reliance on self-report)
- Single-Source Labelling (assigning persona from one episode; missing context-dependent shifts)
- Moralisation & Scapegoating (turning persona diagnosis into blame rather than intervention design)
- Interaction Blindness (analysing personas individually; missing dyadic/coalitional dynamics)
- Positional Invisibility (different roles experience radically different personas; unbalanced sampling)
- Contextual Invisibility (persona only appears in specific contexts; generalisation errors mislead)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Theatre or narrative mismatch | Framework of Communications; Framework of Narratives | Consistency testing (words vs actions); channel-specific performance analysis | Compare across contexts and time; run stress tests; check historical patterns; identify triggers. |
| Hidden sabotage or malevolence | Framework of Risk; Framework of Governance | Anomaly detection; access audits; separation of duties testing | Run safeguard audit; implement protected reporting; escalate to incident protocols. |
| Positional blindness (different levels see different personas) | Framework of Systems; Framework of Evidence | Multi-level stakeholder sampling; two-key review protocols | Institutionalise plural perspectives; rotate observers; create safe truth channels; adjust governance cadence. |
| Over-reliance on single persona category | Framework of Incentives; Framework of Power | Multi-category cross-check (esp. motivational + epistemic + hierarchical) | Force 8-category mapping; run incentive audit; test for conflation of functional role with motive. |
| Persona shift under stress (revelation dynamics) | Framework of Risk; Framework of Decisions | Longitudinal history review; trigger mapping; prediction testing | Build trigger map; design context-specific engagement; set monitoring for shift signals; plan re-assessment cadence. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. List focal agents; identify situation type (Crisis/Panic/Adversarial/Routine/Transformation) and stakes.
2. For each focal agent (top 3–5), assign candidate personas in four highest-relevance categories (e.g., Dispositional, Motivational, Relational, Epistemic); note confidence.
3. For top personas, quickly estimate two dimensions: Authenticity (Authentic/Strategic/Performed/Hidden?) and Stability (Fixed/Contextual/Fluid?).
4. Identify obvious persona interactions (Champion vs Blocker, Authority vs Authority); note obvious risks.
5. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan across six meta-categories; shortlist top 2–3 hidden personas or theatre risks; flag for Tier 2 if high-consequence.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. List all focal agents in scope; create persona register template (8 categories × 6 types per agent).
2. For each focal agent, assign candidate personas across all eight categories (I–VIII) with confidence levels; document reasoning.
3. Profile each top persona across five dimensions: Authenticity (Authentic to Hidden), Stability (Fixed to Fluid), Visibility (Declared to Unconscious), Scope (Universal to Situational), Intensity (Defining to Trace).
4. Map all five dynamic patterns (Shifting, Conflict, Interaction, Evolution, Revelation); identify triggers, mediators, and monitoring points.
5. For each focal agent, assess three interface types: Observation & Inference (detection methods, triangulation, tests), Engagement & Influence (what shifts stance, incentives, complementary personas), Governance & Safeguarding (controls, escalation, protected reporting).
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-relevance Hidden (essentialism, theatre capture, positional invisibility, contextual blindness), identify mechanism, detectability, consequence, and detection/remediation moves.
7. Design interventions: engagement strategies per persona, team composition, safeguards, monitoring indicators, re-assessment cadence; document residual Unknowns and escalation triggers.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add shortlist of persona-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag, mechanism, detectability, consequence, suspected locus. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record persona classifications, dimensional assessments, dynamic pattern presence/absence, evidence quality, provenance, triangulation sources. | After persona profiling and validation; note gaps (e.g., missing multi-context sampling, weak triangulation, over-reliance on self-report). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about hidden personas, persona shifts under stress, theatre vs authenticity; propose tests (stress tests, prediction tests, history review, interaction observation). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request behavioural history, multi-source interviews, interaction records, channel-specific comms, crisis/stress episodes, role definitions, incentive structures. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Communications and Evidence frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we profiled all focal agents across eight persona categories, assessed key dimensions and dynamics, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, validated personas through multi-context evidence and triangulation, and escalated any high-consequence hidden personas (theatre, sabotage, positional blindness) to Tier 2/3 or downstream frameworks (Governance, Risk, Communications)?
