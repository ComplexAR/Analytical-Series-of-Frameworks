## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Time framework identifies the temporal structure of a scenario across six meta-categories (Temporal Direction, Periodicity, Duration, Sequence, Coupling, Reflexivity); characterizes time-dependent dynamics (lags, delays, accumulation, phase transitions, lock-in); maps temporal horizons and time-scale mismatches; and surfaces temporal Hiddens (long-term invisibility, lag blindness, temporal lock-in, path dependency). It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Intervention Register with time-level intervention sequencing and temporal bottleneck analysis, enabling detection of time-dependent blindness and long-lag invisible dynamics.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lag Blindness | Cause and effect separated by long delays; connection invisible; delayed consequences attributed to unrelated causes. | Lag between action and consequence exceeds attention span; short-term monitoring misses long-lag effects; linearity assumed. | Decisions based on wrong causal attribution; interventions target wrong cause; responsibility gaps. |
| Temporal Lock-in & Path Dependency | Early choices constrain later options; past decisions persist despite changed circumstances; flexibility decreases over time. | Sunk costs; switching costs increase; increasing returns to adoption; institutional inertia; psychological commitment. | Misalignment accumulates; stagnation; crisis required for major change; alternatives foreclosed. |
| Temporal Horizon Mismatch | Horizon of authority/decision mismatches horizon of consequences; short-term incentives drive long-term harm. | Incentive systems reward short-term; leadership turnover disrupts long-term commitment; metrics emphasize immediate outcomes. | Sacrifice long-term for short-term; accumulating debt; delayed crisis. |
| Accumulation & Threshold Crossing | Changes accumulate slowly; threshold approaches undetected; rapid phase transition when threshold crossed; "suddenly" fails. | Change rate slow relative to attention threshold; monitoring infrequent; early warning signals weak; feedback delays. | Black swan event; crisis appears sudden when actually gradual; prevention window missed. |
| Temporal Fragmentation | Temporal coupling is weak; events treated as unrelated when causally linked across time; narrative breaks into episodes. | Attention bounded; time divided into project/planning cycles; causation assumed synchronous not asynchronous. | Misunderstanding of cumulative effects; interventions miss cross-temporal dependencies; learning blocked. |
| Reverse Causality & Teleology | Future anticipated state is treated as explaining current behavior; retroactive narrative explains what-was as inevitable. | Narrative sense-making; rationalization; teleological thinking; self-fulfilling prophecies; path-dependent justification. | Overconfidence in predictions; missed alternatives; inability to adjust when prediction wrong. |
| Temporal Inversion | Downstream consequences treated as upstream causes; outcome-dependent reasoning; causal arrow reversed. | Hindsight bias; outcome bias; correlation-as-causation; narrative reversal. | False diagnoses; wrong intervention targets; root causes missed. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What long-term or slow-moving phenomena are below the attention threshold; what changes are invisible on short timescales? |
| II (Systemic) | How do temporal lags and delays couple with institutions and incentives; what long-lag feedback loops are unmapped? |
| III (Informational) | What evidence of long-lag effects exists; what monitoring systems are too short-timescale to detect slow change? |
| IV (Temporal) | What temporal locks and path dependencies are hardening; at what point is reversal still possible; when does flexibility loss become irreversible? |
| V (Relational) | Whose temporal horizon is dominant in decisions; whose long-term interests are discounted; what power imbalances hide temporal costs? |
| VI (Ontological) | What temporal structures are not yet conceivable; what emergent temporal patterns are unnameable in current frameworks? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Lag Blindness (long delays between cause and effect hide causation)
- Temporal Lock-in (early choices constrain later options; flexibility lost)
- Horizon Mismatch (decision horizon mismatches consequence horizon)
- Accumulation Blindness (slow accumulation invisible until threshold crossed)
- Temporal Fragmentation (causation across time treated as unrelated)
- Reflexive Loops (anticipated futures drive present behavior; self-fulfilling prophecies)
- Temporal Inversion (effects misattributed to causes or vice versa)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lag blindness or long-delay causation | Framework of Causation; Framework of Evidence | Causal lag mapping; long-horizon evidence audit | Trace causal chains across time; identify delay points; strengthen long-lag monitoring. |
| Temporal lock-in or path dependency | Framework of Interventions; Framework of Learning | Change sequencing; transition design; flexibility preservation | Identify lock-in points; design exit pathways; preserve optionality. |
| Accumulation crossing threshold | Framework of Hiddens; Framework of Risk | Threshold monitoring; early warning design; leading indicators | Install accumulation monitoring; establish trigger points; design graduated response. |
| Temporal horizon mismatch | Framework of Governance; Framework of Decisions | Authority-consequence alignment; incentive redesign | Align decision horizons with consequence horizons; extend performance metrics. |
| Reflexive temporal loop | Framework of Systems; Framework of Learning | Feedback loop analysis; learning cycle design | Map reflexive loops; test predictions; adjust based on error. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify temporal horizons: list key events, phases, and timescales in scenario; identify what is measured on what timescale.
2. Assess temporal direction assumption: Is time assumed linear, cyclic, branching, or emergent; is assumption justified?
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six meta-categories; flag obvious long-lag invisibility, horizon mismatches, and accumulation phenomena.
4. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence temporal Hiddens (lag blindness, lock-in, threshold crossing); note if temporal lock-in or path dependency is suspected; flag for escalation.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Temporal structure inventory: map all focal timescales, phases, lifecycles, and periodic structures; record temporal direction assumptions.
2. Causal lag map: for each key causal chain, identify lags between cause and effect; estimate delay duration; assess observability at different timescales.
3. Temporal lock-in analysis: identify early choices that constrain later options; estimate switching costs and timing of irreversibility; map flexibility loss trajectory.
4. Temporal coupling map: identify where temporal delays couple with institutions, incentives, or other systems; sketch long-lag feedback loops.
5. Horizon mismatch audit: record decision horizons (planning cycle, leadership tenure, incentive timescale); record consequence horizons (when effects appear); identify mismatches.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence temporal Hidden, identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; propose temporal sensitivity tests.
7. Limits register: document what cannot be temporally reversed; record irreversibility points; flag reflexive temporal effects (where analysis and intervention reshape temporal dynamics).

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add temporal Hiddens with meta-category tag (Direction, Periodicity, Duration, Sequence, Coupling, Reflexivity); mechanism; consequence. | After Tier 1 scan; escalate lock-in and threshold crossing. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record temporal observations, lag estimates, accumulation rates, lock-in timing; note evidence quality and gaps for long-lag phenomena. | After temporal structure inventory and causal lag mapping. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about lags, lock-in points, threshold locations, reflexive loops; propose temporal-sensitivity tests. | Identified temporal Unknowns; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request long-horizon evidence; request accumulation rate data; request alternative temporal models; request stakeholder perceptions of temporal structure. | To reduce temporal Unknowns; coordinate with Evidence and Causation frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we mapped focal timescales and temporal direction, identified long-lag causation and lock-in points, assessed horizon mismatches, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and designed time-appropriate intervention sequencing?
