## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Realities framework surfaces the deep assumptions embedded in scenario analysis about what is real, constructed, knowable, and inevitable; identifies six meta-categories of reality claims (Ontological, Epistemological, Causal, Temporal, Normative, Reflexive) and the types within each; tests reality claims against evidence and alternative ontologies; and flags where divergent reality assumptions drive disagreement, miscommunication, or blindness. It populates the Hiddens Register and Evidence Ledger with reality-assumption challenges, enabling detection of paradigm-driven blindness, category errors, and false inevitability.

## Section 4: Condensed Core Taxonomy

| Meta-category | Core types (names only) | Diagnostic question (1 sentence) |
|---|---|---|
| I. Ontological Claims | Natural vs Constructed, Objective vs Subjective, Given vs Made, Essential vs Contingent, Real vs Apparent | What in this scenario is assumed to be real vs constructed; are those assumptions defensible or hidden? |
| II. Epistemological Claims | Observable vs Hidden, Knowable vs Unknowable, Provable vs Speculative, Verifiable vs Assumed, Accessible vs Forbidden | What is assumed to be knowable vs unknowable from available positions; are those limits justified? |
| III. Causal Claims | Determinism vs Indeterminism, Necessity vs Contingency, Single-Cause vs Multi-Causation, Agency vs Mechanism, Direction vs Bidirectional | What causal structure is assumed (deterministic, probabilistic, agentic); is it justified? |
| IV. Temporal Claims | Linear vs Cyclic, Progressive vs Stable, Reversible vs Irreversible, Universal Time vs Local Time, Durational vs Eternal | What time structure (linear progress, cycles, reversibility) is assumed; is it context-appropriate? |
| V. Normative Claims | Inherent Value vs Assigned Value, Natural Justice vs Constructed Justice, Objective Good vs Subjective Preference, Intrinsic vs Instrumental | What is assumed to be valuable or obligatory by nature vs by design; whose values are embedded? |
| VI. Reflexive Claims | Reality Independent vs Constructed-by-Analysis, Truth Absolute vs Contextual, Framework-Neutral vs Framework-Dependent, Observation Transparent vs Constitutive | How is reality assumed to relate to the analysis and observer; are frameworks transparent or constitutive? |

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hidden Reality Assumption | Stakeholders diverge on basic facts; disagreement appears factual but reflects ontological differences; "facts" contradict. | Ontological assumptions (what counts as real) differ; not articulated or questioned; treated as obvious. | Irresolvable disagreement; talking past each other; solutions fail because wrong problem is named. |
| False Inevitability | Something treated as inevitable/natural that could be otherwise; constraints treated as immutable; alternatives invisible. | Assumptions about what is given vs made are unexamined; naturalization hides constructed character; power obscures choices. | Path dependency; missed opportunities; resistance to change; alternative designs never attempted. |
| Category Error | Phenomenon treated as real/objective when it is constructed/subjective or vice versa; phenomena at different levels confused. | Analyst applies ontological category from one domain to another without checking; boundaries between domains are blurred. | Intervention mismatches reality structure; explanations talk past the actual mechanism; solutions ineffective. |
| Paradigm Blindness | Alternative reality assumptions completely invisible; paradigm so dominant that alternatives are not even conceivable. | Paradigm permeates all sense-making; alternative ontologies marginalized or excluded; expertise silos reinforce paradigm. | Black swans; surprises from alternative paradigms; inability to imagine responses until too late. |
| Epistemological Closure | Reality assumed knowable when it is fundamentally unknowable from available positions; limits of knowledge unexamined. | Confidence in measurability/observability treated as fact rather than assumption; principled unknowability confused with mere difficulty. | Over-confidence; decisions made on invisible foundations; surprises when unknowable-in-principle becomes visible. |
| Normative-Factual Collapse | Values assumed to be facts (or vice versa); what ought to be treated as what is; moral claims hidden in factual language. | Analyst conflates is/ought; power actors impose preferred ontology as if obvious; discourse analysis ignored. | Unexamined value imposition; covert normative choices; legitimacy deficits when values become explicit. |
| Reflexive Naivety | Reality treated as independent of analysis; framework treated as transparent mirror of what-is; observer position invisible. | Analysis assumed neutral; frameworks assumed to discover reality rather than construct it; reflexivity denied. | Paradigm assumptions go unexamined; alternatives foreclosed; framework bias hidden; credibility challenged when reflexivity surfaces. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What ontological assumptions (what counts as real) are salient vs invisible from common positions; what is taken for granted? |
| II (Systemic) | How do reality assumptions couple with institutions, incentives, and power; what alternatives are structurally suppressed? |
| III (Informational) | What evidence would challenge current reality assumptions; what evidence is deemed inadmissible or invisible? |
| IV (Temporal) | What reality assumptions are products of particular historical moments; what alternatives exist across time? |
| V (Relational) | Whose reality assumptions are dominant; whose ontologies are marginalized; what power asymmetries hide alternative realities? |
| VI (Ontological) | What categories of reality cannot be represented in current ontology; what entities are foreclosed; what is unthinkable? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Unexamined Ontological Assumptions (what counts as real goes unquestioned)
- False Inevitability (constructed realities treated as given/natural)
- Category Error (phenomena miscategorized across ontological boundaries)
- Paradigm Blindness (alternative ontologies invisible or dismissed)
- Epistemological Overconfidence (reality assumed fully knowable)
- Naturalization (value-laden claims hidden as factual)
- Reflexive Denial (framework treated as transparent rather than constitutive)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Irresolvable disagreement on basic facts | Framework of Perspectives; Framework of Hiddens | Ontological assumption audit; alternative paradigm mapping | Surface reality assumptions; test against evidence; engage alternative paradigms. |
| False inevitability or path lock-in | Framework of Interventions; Framework of Learning | Alternative history; design space exploration | Question naturalness; sketch alternatives; test feasibility. |
| Category error or ontological confusion | Framework of Dimensions; Framework of Diagnosis | Category boundary audit; tier-level reframing | Clarify category boundaries; apply correct ontological tier. |
| Paradigm blindness or black swan risk | Framework of Hiddens (Tier 2); Framework of Uncertainties | Paradigm diversity scan; alternative-ontology stress testing | Engage alternative paradigms; stress-test against assumptions. |
| Normative-factual collapse | Framework of Values; Framework of Legitimacy | Value audit; fairness/legitimacy analysis | Make values explicit; separate factual claims from normative choices. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. List focal reality claims in scenario (what is assumed to be real, inevitable, knowable); tag each by meta-category (I–VI).
2. For 2–3 highest-stakes reality claims, ask: what is the alternative assumption; is current assumption justified; what evidence would shift it?
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: flag obvious reality assumptions that are unexamined or controversial; note if different stakeholders make different reality claims.
4. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence reality divergences; note whether disagreement is factual or ontological; flag for escalation if paradigm clash is suspected.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Reality claim inventory: list all salient reality claims in scenario; assign each to meta-category (I–VI) and core type; note stakeholder variance.
2. Evidence audit: for each reality claim, record supporting evidence, contradicting evidence, evidence quality, gaps, and alternative interpretations.
3. Alternative reality test: for each contested reality claim, sketch plausible alternative assumption; test against evidence; identify what would falsify each.
4. Paradigm mapping: identify underlying paradigms (scientific, humanistic, indigenous, spiritual, etc.) and how they structure reality assumptions; assess paradigm coherence and conflict.
5. Reflexivity check: examine how analysis framework itself shapes which realities are visible; test framework assumptions; sketch alternative analytical frames.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence reality assumption, identify mechanism of invisibility, detectability, consequence; assign detection/remediation interfaces.
7. Limits register: document what is philosophically unknowable; record Gödel-like limits on self-reference; flag reflexivity blindness in own analysis.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add reality-assumption Hiddens with meta-category tag (Ontological, Epistemological, Causal, Temporal, Normative, Reflexive); mechanism; consequence. | After Tier 1 scan; escalate paradigm conflicts and reflexivity issues. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record reality claims, supporting/contradicting evidence, quality, gaps, alternative interpretations; note paradigm-dependent evidence admissibility. | After evidence audit; flag evidence deemed inadmissible by different paradigms. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about reality structure; propose tests (falsifying conditions, alternative paradigm stress-testing, reflexivity probes). | Identified Unknowns and contested reality claims; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request evidence for neglected reality assumptions; request alternative paradigm literature; request reflexivity audits from outside perspectives. | To reduce Unknowns; coordinate with Evidence and Perspectives frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we surfaced focal reality assumptions, tested them against evidence, identified alternative ontologies, checked for paradigm conflict and reflexivity blindness, and escalated high-consequence reality divergences?
