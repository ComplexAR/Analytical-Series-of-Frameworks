# Introduction

## What is the Framework of Personas?

A persona is not an agent's essence; it is a context-activated mode of engagement—a recognisable pattern of stance, role-performance, and posture that shapes how an agent relates to knowledge, action, motives, relationships, thinking, time, function, and authority. The same agent adopts different personas across different situations, audiences, stakes, and power relationships. A person who is a trusted collaborator in routine conditions may become a defensive blocker in crisis or a silent observer when their interests are threatened. A leader who champions change when it aligns with their incentives may become its subtle saboteur when costs shift to them. These are not character flaws or inconsistencies; they are predictable, context-driven modes that can be mapped, tested, and engaged.

This framework addresses a fundamental analytical gap. Standard stakeholder analysis identifies who holds what roles and formal positions. Power and incentives analysis identifies what rewards and constraints shape behaviour. But neither answers the question: **how will this person actually position themselves and engage in this situation, and what is likely to shift them?** The answer lies in understanding personas—the eight distinct dimensions through which humans relate to complex scenarios.

Why this matters: personas determine outcomes. A team composed of drivers and executors without evaluators crashes hard when early signals are missed. A transformation programme staffed with supporters but led by a novice in an adversarial context risks collapse from hidden resistance. A crisis response staffed with rule-followers rather than deciders grinds to paralysis. A governance structure that assumes good faith in the presence of malevolent actors enables systemic theft. Persona blindness is expensive.

## Why does the Framework of Personas matter for Hiddens work?

Persona performance is itself a form of hiddenness. Theatre—where people present one face publicly and a different one privately—is pervasive. Secrecy about true motives is common. Positional invisibility ensures that the realities visible to frontline workers differ radically from those seen by distant leadership. And narrative capture can conceal incentive-driven behaviour beneath seemingly principled language.

This framework surfaces several classes of Hiddens:

**Hidden-8 (Framing and narrative capture)** operates when persona labels foreground certain levers and hide others. One agent frames behaviour as "principled resistance"; another sees "obstruction". One reads commitment as "authentic support"; another sees "compliance theatre". The same behaviours are invisible or damaging depending on which persona interpretation has captured the narrative.

**Hidden-21 (Positional invisibility)** means different roles experience radically different personas from the same person. A charismatic decision-maker seen as "bold and decisive" by peers may appear to subordinates as "dismissive and autocratic". A consensus-builder appears to competitors as "weak and indecisive". The persona depends on where you sit.

**Hidden-24 (Contextual invisibility)** arises because personas activate and deactivate with situation. A behaviourally-stable persona in routine conditions may flip radically in crisis. Behaviours that appear cooperative in private may reverse in public performance. Without understanding situational activation, you misclassify and mispredict.

**Hidden-11 (Secrecy and concealment)** operates when agents deliberately hide true motives or roles. Saboteurs perform loyalty while systematically undermining. False experts project confidence without competence. Pragmatic survivors present alignment while preserving personal optionality. These personas are strategically hidden and only surface under stress or when incentives shift.

Without this framework, these Hiddens remain latent. With it, persona analysis becomes a repeatable, testable discipline that flags mislabelling, exposure points, and engagement risks early.

## What does this framework produce?

The Framework of Personas operationalises analysis through six core elements:

1. **A taxonomy of 48 persona types** organised into eight meta-categories (Epistemic, Dispositional, Motivational, Relational, Cognitive, Temporal, Functional, Hierarchical) that recur across contexts and scales, allowing rapid initial classification and comparison.

2. **Five cross-cutting dimensions** (Authenticity, Stability, Visibility, Scope, Intensity) that profile any persona and explain why different observers classify the same person differently.

3. **Dynamics patterns** (Shifting, Conflict, Interaction, Evolution, Revelation) that describe how personas change, amplify risks, or unlock progress—enabling detection of transitions and early warning of instability.

4. **Three interface types** (Observation & Inference, Engagement & Influence, Governance & Safeguarding) that systematise how you detect personas, interact with them to shift positions, and manage high-risk patterns.

5. **Hiddens source analysis and tiered Hiddens scans** (Tier 1 six-category audit, Tier 2 mechanism-specific deepening, Tier 3 full-spectrum escalation) that surface systematic vulnerabilities in persona analysis itself (projection, essentialism, theatre capture, positional blindness).

6. **A seven-step application protocol** that moves from focal agent definition through persona mapping, evidence validation, dimensional profiling, Hiddens scanning, and intervention design.

The framework populates standard registers (Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, Hypothesis/Test Backlog, Information Requests) that integrate with the broader Analytical Series investigation workflow. It is designed for iterative use: personas are mapped, engagement is designed, outcomes are observed, personas are re-scanned when conditions shift, and safeguards are tightened as evidence accumulates.

## How to use this framework

Invoke this framework whenever focal actors' behaviour is material to outcomes—which is most scenarios involving change, risk, negotiation, or governance. It is most valuable early (in scenario intake, to understand likely resistance and support patterns), during decision gates (to validate whether your stakeholder model still holds), and when interventions stall or fail (often signalling persona misreading or hidden resistance).

The framework operates in both Rapid Run Mode (light-touch persona assignment, quick interface spotting, basic engagement next-steps) and Investigative Run Mode (full dimensional profiling, evidence-gated inference, detailed Hiddens scanning, multi-stakeholder triangulation).

Default execution is Light Depth Framework Execution: assign personas in all eight categories, profile two to three dimensions, identify obvious triggering conditions and interactions, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan (six-category visibility audit), and produce an engagement plan. Escalate to Full Depth Framework Execution when: consequence is high, personas are contested or hidden, safeguarding risks are plausible, adversarial conditions apply, or you are approaching a triggering transition (e.g., from routine to crisis).

The framework's companion prompt discipline rules (OG v2.5, §D.3) require tagging all persona claims as Fact, Interpretation, Assumption, or Unknown, and explicitly recording disagreements, uncertainties, and residual unknowns rather than collapsing them into false confidence. For LLM execution, see §1.6 for the complete LLM integration specification, standard registers, and copy-ready prompts. The framework is designed to be directly executable by an LLM given a scenario, operating guide, and disciplined structure.

# 1. Core Premise & Position in the Analytical Series

## 1.1 Core Premise (narrative)

Agents adopt personas: recognisable patterns of stance, role-performance, and posture that shape how they engage with knowledge, action, motives, relationships, cognition, time, function, and authority. Personas are **not** an agent's essence; they are context-activated modes that can be stable, transient, visible, hidden, or strategic. Different agents can adopt the same persona, and the same agent can shift personas across different conditions.

This framework enables systematic prediction and intervention by:
- mapping personas across a complete taxonomy (8 meta-categories, 48 types),
- characterising each active persona across five dimensions (authenticity, stability, visibility, scope, intensity),
- modelling dynamics (shifting, conflict, interaction, evolution, revelation),
- anticipating activation by situation type and stressor, and
- designing engagement and safeguarding interventions.

Because persona analysis is vulnerable to projection, secrecy, positional invisibility, and theatre, Section 8 includes a mandatory **tiered Hiddens cross-check** for non-trivial or high-stakes work. The framework produces a persona register per focal agent with dimension profiles, dynamics notes, and engagement/safeguarding recommendations that integrate into broader governance and decision structures.

## 1.3 Definitions, Scope, and Non-Goals
- **Persona (operational)**: a context-activated pattern of stance and posture that shapes perception, action, and interaction.
- **Not**: a clinical diagnosis, personality assessment, or substitute for legal/HR processes; not a moral label.
- **In scope**:
  - Eight meta-categories and 48 persona types (v2.0 structure)
  - Five dimensions of any persona
  - Five persona dynamics and situational activation patterns
  - Three interface types for inference, engagement, and safeguarding
  - Hiddens source analysis + tiered Hiddens scan (Tier 1–3)
  - Seven-step application protocol
- **Out of scope**:
  - Full psychometrics or clinical trait models
  - Detailed organisational role design beyond persona-functional implications
  - Full intervention catalogue (use Interventions framework)
  - Personnel discipline or HR decisions (use Responsibility framework)

### 1.6.3 Routing triggers
- Behaviour prediction requires understanding how agents will position themselves (not just formal roles)
- Resistance or support is being misread; compliance claims lack evidence; stakeholder alignment is assumed
- Agents display inconsistent behaviour across contexts, audiences, or time; persona switching is suspected
- Engagement design needs calibration to actor posture (not just to formal position or stated motive)
- Theatre, secrecy, or strategic silence are suspected; "what people say" diverges from "what people do"
- Team composition or leader-follower dynamics are sources of failure or misalignment
- Authority pathologies, followership problems, or governance breakdown are present
- Safeguarding against bad-actor risk, sabotage, or covert resistance is required

## 2.2 Meta-Category Descriptions (recommended)
- **I. Epistemic** — how the agent relates to knowledge, evidence, and uncertainty; confidence calibration and learning stance.
- **II. Dispositional** — stance toward action: support, critique, resistance, or covert undermining; change adoption posture.
- **III. Motivational** — what drives the agent's actions: prosocial, institutional, self-interested, transactional, opportunistic, or harmful.
- **IV. Relational** — positioning toward others: authority, collaboration, subordination, competition, mediation, outsiderhood.
- **V. Cognitive** — dominant thinking pattern and lens: tribal, avoidant, language-captive, theatre-focused, systems-oriented, reductionist.
- **VI. Temporal** — orientation to time: tradition, preservation, reform, revolution, future, present.
- **VII. Functional** — function served in execution: driving, executing, coordinating, creating, evaluating, connecting.
- **VIII. Hierarchical** — relationship to authority: leader and follower modes (engagement, critique, compliance, pragmatism).

## 5.1 Dynamic Patterns Table (mandatory)
| # | Dynamic pattern | Signature | Mechanism | Why it matters | Typical mitigations |

# 7. Relationship to Other Frameworks (Integration)

## 7.1 Primary Integration Links (recommended)

Personas is a **G3 (Agency)** framework with a **G4 (Meaning)** secondary orientation. It is most effective when used as a *behavioural interpretation and engagement-design layer* on top of agent inventories, structural drivers (power/incentives), and evidence hygiene.

Primary integration links (high-frequency):
- **Agents**: establish the agent set; personas are applied per focal agent or role.
- **Incentives + Power**: distinguish constraint-driven behaviour from strategic performance; identify who can punish disclosure.
- **Culture and Norms + Legitimacy + Values**: calibrate cue interpretation and identify legitimacy/value conflicts presented as "personality".
- **Narratives + Communications**: detect theatre, signalling, and channel-dependent persona performance.
- **Evidence + Uncertainties + Hiddens**: keep persona inference evidence-gated; treat persona performance as a potential hiding mechanism; escalate when stakes/adversarial conditions apply.
- **Governance + Responsibility + Risk + Interventions**: translate persona findings into controls, role design, safeguarding, and intervention sequencing.

### 7.1.1 Integration contract
- **Inputs expected**: agent list and roles; situation type; evidence base and history; incentive/power map; governance/decision rights; cultural norms; system interfaces and dependencies.
- **Outputs provided**: persona register; dimension and dynamics profile; predicted shifts under stress; engagement and safeguarding interventions; monitoring indicators; Hiddens shortlist and escalation triggers.

# 8. Hiddens Source Analysis & Cross-Check (mandatory)

## 8.1 Hiddens Source Analysis (framework-specific)
| # | Generator name | Pattern (how analysis fails) | Remedy (how to correct) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Persona essentialism | Treating personas as fixed traits ('this is who they are') rather than context-dependent modes. | Use the five dimensions; explicitly model triggers and shifting; treat personas as hypotheses, not identities. |
| 2 | Attribution error | Explaining behaviour solely by persona while ignoring constraints, incentives, and situational activation. | Run incentive and constraint checks; map situational type; test alternative explanations. |
| 3 | Over-reliance on self-report | Accepting declared personas without behavioural verification; being captured by theatre and language. | Triangulate with actions, history, and third parties; run prediction tests; watch for inconsistency under stress. |
| 4 | Single-source labelling | Assigning a persona based on one episode or one observer's view. | Collect multi-context evidence; use positional sampling; compare private/public behaviour; document confidence level. |
| 5 | Moralisation and scapegoating | Turning persona diagnosis into blame ('villains') rather than a tool for intervention design. | Separate intent, effect, and context; focus on control points and safeguards; use governance escalation criteria. |
| 6 | Ignoring interaction effects | Analysing personas individually and missing dyadic/coalitional dynamics (Champion–Blocker, Authority–Authority). | Map interactions and team composition; use mediator roles; design counterweights and complements. |
| 7 | Confusing function with motive | Assuming functional roles (Executor, Coordinator) imply prosocial motives or epistemic integrity. | Cross-check across categories; explicitly map motivational and epistemic personas in parallel. |
| 8 | Hidden-adversary neglect | Assuming good faith when incentives favour sabotage or malevolence; failing to run safeguards early. | Use revelation and anomaly checks; apply separation of duties; create protected reporting and audit trails. |
| 9 | Category truncation | Only scanning a subset of persona categories (e.g., epistemic + dispositional) and missing authority/functional drivers. | Map across all eight categories; use the protocol's full persona register before narrowing. |
| 10 | Hiddens omission | Failing to run systematic Hiddens scan; missing projection, secrecy, positional, and perspectival invisibilities. | Execute Tier 1 Hiddens scan for every non-trivial case; build Tier 2 hidden-type shortlist with detection/remediation. |

## 8.2 Hiddens Six-Category Scan (Tier 1 – invariant)
| Hiddens meta-category | Why it may be active here | Typical symptoms | Default checks to run |
|---|---|---|---|
| I. Perceptual | Observers over-index on salient behaviours and under-sample contexts; personas inferred from sparse evidence. | Mislabeling; overconfidence; stereotyping; 'one incident defines the agent'. | Multi-context sampling; independent observers; explicit confidence ratings; prediction tests. |
| II. Systemic | Group narratives and institutional incentives stabilise persona stories (hero/villain) regardless of evidence. | Scapegoating; role lock-in; theatre; 'we always know who the blocker is'. | Narrative audit; incentive analysis; counterfactual review; update rules for attributions. |
| III. Informational | Key evidence sits in private channels; some personas are deliberately concealed (saboteur/malevolent). | Conflicting accounts; missing records; sudden 'revelations'; information asymmetry. | Triangulation; provenance checks; protected interviews; access and audit trail review. |
| IV. Temporal | Personas shift with stress and evolve with experience; patterns only visible longitudinally. | Surprise switching; delayed burnout; apparent hypocrisy; 'they changed overnight'. | History review; lag/trigger mapping; cadence for re-assessment; early warning indicators. |
| V. Relational | Power and position shape what is visible and what can be said; authority personas can suppress truth. | Silence; conformity; retaliation; captured narratives; unchallenged authority claims. | Positional sampling; two-key reviews; safe reporting; power–accountability alignment audit. |
| VI. Ontological | New or hybrid personas are miscategorised, or categories are absent. | Persistent confusion; disputes over 'what kind of person this is'; repeated mis-engagement. | Category creation exercise; compare with analog cases; update taxonomy with mapped subtypes. |

## 8.4 Embedded Hiddens Micro-Protocol (standard prompts)
1. **Run Tier 1 scan across all six Hiddens meta-categories** (assume hiddens exist; do not require evidence).
2. **Shortlist relevant Hidden types (Tier 2)** and rate detectability, agency, and consequence for each.
3. **Assign at least one detection and one remediation move per high-consequence hidden**; map interaction chains (e.g., Projection → Mirage → scapegoating).
4. **Re-run hiddens source analysis** and document residual unknowns and escalation triggers.

## 8.5 Escalation Rule (Tier 3 – full Hiddens run)
Escalate to a full 30-type Hiddens scan + detection/remediation matrix mapping if any apply:
- **High consequence** (safety, systemic resilience, regulatory exposure, severe harm potential)
- **Adversarial / strategic concealment suspicion** (Saboteur/Malevolent plausible)
- **Persistent disputes** about intent, legitimacy, or authority
- **Repeated failures** despite engagement attempts
- **Evidence of theatre or narrative capture** masking real behaviour

# 9. Framework Application Protocol (mandatory)

## 9.1 Application Steps Table
| Step # | Step name | Action | Outputs / artefacts |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Define focal agents and situation | Identify focal agents; classify situational context (crisis/panic/adversarial/routine/transformation); set stakes and horizon. | Agent list; situation type; objectives; assumptions and constraints; evidence plan. |
| 2 | Map personas across all eight categories | For each focal agent, assign candidate personas in each category (I–VIII) with confidence levels. | Persona register per agent (8×6 shortlist); confidence ratings; competing hypotheses. |
| 3 | Validate with evidence and tests | Use detection methods and verification strategies (triangulation, prediction tests, stress tests, history review) to validate or revise personas. | Evidence log; validated personas; disconfirmed labels; predicted-next-action tests and results. |
| 4 | Characterise dimensions and dynamics | Profile personas by authenticity, stability, visibility, scope, intensity; assess dynamics (shifting, conflict, interaction, evolution, revelation). | Dimension profiles; trigger map; interaction map; situational activation hypothesis. |
| 5 | Run Hiddens source analysis + Hiddens cross-check | Apply hiddens source analysis list; perform Tier 1 Hiddens scan; shortlist Tier 2 hidden types and assign detection/remediation moves. | Hiddens source analysis results; Hiddens shortlist; escalation triggers; residual unknowns register. |
| 6 | Design interventions and monitoring | Design engagement and safeguarding interventions; compose teams and counterweights; set governance, indicators, and review cadence. | Intervention plan (by persona); safeguards for bad-actor risk; monitoring indicators; re-assessment cadence. |

## 9.2 Standard Deliverables (recommended)
- **Minimum deliverable (1–2 pages)**:
  - Focal agents + situation type + stakes
  - Persona register (8-category shortlist) with confidence ratings
  - Dimension profiles + dynamics/trigger notes
  - Engagement plan (counterweights, negotiation moves, safeguards)
  - Hiddens shortlist with detection/remediation actions and escalation triggers

- **Full deliverable pack**:
  - Interaction and coalition map (who amplifies whom)
  - Persona risk register (sabotage/malevolence; authority capture; conformity/silence)
  - Monitoring indicators + re-assessment cadence
  - Intervention portfolio linked to Incentives/Power/Governance frameworks
  - Residual Unknowns register
  - Closure note and escalation recommendation
