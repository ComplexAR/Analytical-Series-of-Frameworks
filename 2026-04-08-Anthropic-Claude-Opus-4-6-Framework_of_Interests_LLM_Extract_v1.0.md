## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Framework of Interests identifies what agents fundamentally need, want, or care about beneath their stated positions and demands. It classifies interests into six meta-categories (material, procedural, identity, relational, temporal, institutional) with thirty core types; profiles them across five cross-cutting dimensions (materiality level, consciousness, sustainability, scope, transparency); maps interest conflicts and coalitions; and surfaces the hidden interests that explain behaviour diverging from stated positions, negotiation failures, and persistent resistance. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with interest-specific blindnesses and detection/remediation interfaces, enabling downstream frameworks to design interventions addressing real rather than apparent motivations.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interest Alignment & Coalition Formation | Rapid coalition formation around clusters of shared interests; coordinated action by previously separate actors; alignment statements | Agents with aligned interests identify common cause; coordination becomes rational strategy; visibility increases coalition attraction | Coalition stability depends on interest alignment; if underlying interests diverge, coalition becomes fragile; address shared interests to stabilise or break coalition |
| Interest Conflict Escalation | Positions harden; actors bring in allies; public statements become more extreme; willingness to compromise declines | Actors become emotionally and publicly invested in position; backing down becomes identity-threatening; group pressure amplifies stakes | Conflict becomes harder to resolve as escalation proceeds; early negotiation is more productive than late-stage mediation |
| Hidden Interest Revelation | Behaviour shifts when incentives change; resistance appears disproportionate to stated stakes; contradictions between statements and actions | Pressure or failure activates hidden interests; behaviour reveals what is actually at stake beneath surface positions | Run interest-first analysis when behaviour diverges from incentives; probe for hidden interests explaining resistance and persistence |
| Interest Drift & Goal Shift | Actor's priorities shift without explanation; resistance that was absent now appears; stated goals change over time | Contexts change; new interests activate; old interests become satiated; interests are not static across time | Monitor for interest drift; escalate governance and reviews when priorities shift; adapt targets and expectations to changing interest landscape |
| Interest Substitution & Migration | When one path is blocked, agents pursue alternative interests; energy transfers from blocked path to alternative domain | Blocked interests do not disappear; agents find alternative paths to satisfaction; substituted interests may have unintended consequences | Understand full range of interests; anticipate where blocked interests will redirect; be alert to substitution effects in policy and intervention design |
| Interest Amplification Through Framing | Same underlying interest generates different salience/urgency depending on framing; threat-framing amplifies urgency; collective framing amplifies alignment | Framing shapes perception of stakes; threat-language activates urgency; collective language activates group identity and amplifies priority | Pay attention to framing; understand that interest amplification is real and powerful; use framing consciously in design rather than allowing it to be imposed |
| Interest Suppression & Denial | Behaviour reveals interests but actors deny them; rationalisation and narrative reframing mask interests that conflict with self-image | Interests that are identity-threatening are denied or rationalised; admission would contradict self-narrative or group identity | Don't challenge denial directly; work with observed interests; allow actors to maintain face while interests are addressed |
| Interest Cascade Across Levels | Interest satisfaction or frustration at one level creates demands at other levels; individual frustration cascades to institutional level demands | Unaddressed interests at lower levels accumulate; escalation to formal channels; institutional level becomes responsible for addressing aggregated demand | Track cascades across levels; design interventions addressing cascade points; allow resolution across multiple levels simultaneously |
| Temporal Interest Shift | Under crisis, immediate interests (subsistence, security) dominate; as stability increases, higher-order interests (identity, learning, optionality) become salient | Time horizons compress under threat; priorities shift toward immediate security; as threat recedes, future-oriented interests re-activate | Understand actor's temporal location; crisis response differs from steady-state; design transitions from crisis-mode to stable-state interest activation |
| Interest Clustering & Correlation | Some interests activate together; identity interests cluster with procedural interests; relational interests can align with institutional interests | Interest structures have correlation patterns; certain combinations are common in specific contexts and roles | Map interest clusters in your context; don't treat interests as independent; understand correlation structures for coalition and conflict prediction |
| Unconscious-to-Conscious Interest Conversion | Latent interests become conscious through dialogue and naming; resistance reframed when underlying interest is acknowledged | Interests pursued unconsciously remain opaque; naming interests explicitly changes negotiation dynamic from positional to interest-based | Create space for interests to be named; don't force consciousness but enable it; once conscious, interests become negotiable |
| Interest Satiation & Plateau Effects | As interests are satisfied, additional satisfaction produces diminishing returns; interests become latent when sufficiently satisfied; re-activate when threatened | Interest dominance wanes as satisfaction increases; new interests become salient; dormant interests re-activate when satisfaction is threatened | Understand satiation patterns; don't assume constant intensity; design systems detecting interest activation and responding with appropriate remediation |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What interests are invisible to decision-makers because they fall outside typical attention frame or are not salient to those analyzing the scenario? |
| II (Systemic) | How are interest conflicts embedded in system structure but treated as individual problems or intractable personality differences rather than as structural? |
| III (Informational) | What key information about stakes, constraints, or actor understanding is missing that leads to misunderstanding of what is actually driving behaviour? |
| IV (Temporal) | How are interest shifts over time being tracked or missed; are interests being treated as static when they are actually volatile or shifting? |
| V (Relational) | What interests are being hidden because stating them would violate cultural norms, hierarchy rules, or social appropriateness; what interests are pursued covertly? |
| VI (Ontological) | What concepts or language for articulating interests are missing; what interests exist but have no framework for being named or discussed? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Interests-Positions Confusion (treating stated positions as actual interests; solutions address positions while true interests remain unaddressed)
- Strategic Concealment (actors deliberately hiding true interests behind plausible positions to negotiate advantage)
- Interests-Incentives Conflation (attributing behaviour to incentive structures when actual driver is underlying interests conflicting with incentives)
- Unconscious/Latent Interests (interests pursued without explicit awareness; contradiction between stated priorities and revealed behaviour)
- Organisational Interest Aggregation Opacity (individual interests hidden behind organisational position; "the organisation wants X" masks conflicting individual interests)
- Conflicting Interests Within Actors (same actor has multiple competing interests creating internal contradiction and observable inconsistency)
- Positional Blindness (actors in different structural positions face different interest landscapes but assume others operate in their own interest-frame)
- Interests-Values Conflation (behaviour attributed to stated values when actual driver is unstated interests that actors prioritise when forced to choose)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interests-Positions Confusion; solutions addressing stated demands are rejected | Framework of Conflict; Negotiation support | Behaviour-first analysis; resistance pattern mapping | Explicitly distinguish positions from interests; infer interests from behaviour, resistance, and observed choices; design solutions addressing actual interests |
| Strategic Concealment; negotiation fails to surface true interests | Framework of Power; Intelligence analysis | Multi-level detection; coalition analysis; revealed preferences | Use multi-level interviews; analyse behaviour patterns and coalitions; test proposals against revealed priorities; assume misrepresentation in high-stakes contexts |
| Unconscious/Latent Interests; behaviour contradicts stated priorities | Framework of Emotions; Values framework | Interest articulation dialogue; probing questions | Create safe spaces for interests to be surfaced; ask "what would have to be true for you to accept this?"; use behaviour analysis to infer latent interests |
| Interest Conflicts Within Actors; internal contradiction causing vulnerability | Framework of Emotions; Decisions framework | Multi-interest mapping; interest trade-off dialogue | Assume actors have multiple competing interests; ask explicitly about all six interest domains; help actors prioritise when interests conflict |
| Organisational Interest Aggregation; incoherence in organisational positions | Multi-level coordination; Responsibility framework | Multi-level interview and comparison; contradiction detection | Separate individual interests from organisational interests; interview across levels; understand that different roles face different interest landscapes |
| Positional Blindness; inter-level/inter-departmental misunderstanding | Relations framework; Systems framework | Role-specific interest mapping; cross-level dialogue | Map interest landscapes by position; design systems accommodating different positional interests; use multiple-narrative approach making different positions explicit |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Identify the focal actors and their explicitly stated positions and demands; note where stated positions appear to conflict or where resistance appears disproportionate.
2. Map the six interest meta-categories lightly for key actors: note obvious material interests (security, resources), procedural interests (voice, fairness), identity interests (belonging, dignity), and any visible relational or temporal interests.
3. Assess three critical dimensions: Consciousness/Awareness (are interests being stated or inferred from behaviour?), Transparency (are interests openly admitted or strategically concealed?), and Sustainability (are these persistent interests or situational?).
4. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six Hiddens categories lightly; flag obvious visibility failures (interests not in decision-makers' awareness, strategic concealment, interests-positions confusion).
5. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence hidden interests; note detection and remediation interfaces; flag for escalation to Full Depth if strategic concealment or consequence is high.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Conduct full actor inventory with role, authority, resource dependencies, and prior conflict history; map each actor's positional constraints and what different positions demand.
2. Classify interests comprehensively across all six meta-categories for each focal actor; profile each interest on five dimensions (materiality, consciousness, sustainability, scope, transparency); create interest profile matrices.
3. Map interest conflicts and coalitions: identify which actors have aligned interests (coalition drivers), which have cross-cutting interests (internal conflict), which have directly opposed interests (conflict sources).
4. Analyse all twelve interest dynamics: for each focal actor, assess which dynamics are active (alignment, drift, suppression, escalation, substitution, satiation); identify cascade points across levels.
5. Identify interface types: Detection (how interests become visible), Remediation (how interventions address interests), Interaction (how conflicts are negotiated); design specific probes and engagement approaches for high-consequence interests.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each identified Hidden (e.g., interests-positions confusion, strategic concealment, unconscious interests), assess mechanism, detectability, agency, consequence; assign detection and remediation interfaces.
7. Map interest-based coalition stability: understand how coalitions form and what would destabilise or re-align them; generate redesign options addressing underlying interests rather than surface positions.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add shortlist of interest-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag (I–VI), mechanism (e.g., strategic concealment, interests-positions confusion), detectability, consequence, remediation interface pointer. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record interest classifications, dimensional profiles, conflict sources, coalition mappings, evidence quality, information gaps (e.g., what would confirm unconscious interest; what behaviour reveals hidden interest). | After interest mapping and dimensional profiling; note gaps requiring additional evidence. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about interest-driven behaviour, interest drift, coalition stability, and interest-conflict outcomes; propose tests (behaviour analysis, resistance pattern testing, counter-proposal testing, multi-level interview). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request access to behaviour patterns (coalitions, resistance, compliance gaps), communication archives (what is discussed/avoided), prior negotiation records, multi-level interviews revealing different positional interests. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Evidence and Conflict frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified the focal actors, classified their interests across all six meta-categories, assessed critical dimensions (consciousness, transparency, scope), identified interest conflicts and coalitions, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and escalated any high-consequence Unknowns (strategic concealment, unconscious interests) to appropriate frameworks (Hiddens, Conflict, Negotiation)?

## Section 8: Cross-Framework Routing Pointers

### 8a. Upstream Inputs (frameworks commonly feeding into this one)

- Agents & Personas (who the actors are; what are their roles, constraints, and typical motivational profiles?)
- Power & Resources (what can actors enforce; what resources do they control or depend on?)
- Situations Context Classification (what situation type shapes which interests are salient?)
- Culture & Norms (what interests are culturally legitimate to pursue; what interests are suppressed by norms?)
- Values & Beliefs (what do actors profess to care about normatively; how do professed values relate to actual interests?)
- Incentives (what reward structures are in place; how do they align with or conflict with underlying interests?)

### 8b. Downstream Handoffs (frameworks commonly consuming this framework's outputs)

- Framework of Conflict (interest-based coalition mapping; root cause of conflict analysis; adversarial opposition mapping)
- Framework of Decisions (how interest structures shape decision options and actor preferences)
- Framework of Negotiations (interest-based rather than positional negotiation design; coalition and trade-off analysis)
- Framework of Interventions (designing interventions addressing actual interests rather than surface positions; interest-accommodating governance)
- Framework of Governance (governance structures that represent or exclude interests; accountability allocation aligned with interest satisfaction)
- Framework of Diagnosis (explaining persistent failures and resistance through interest-driven analysis)
