## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Relations framework maps the network of relationships (dyadic, group, organizational, inter-organizational) that structure a scenario; classifies relationship types across six meta-categories (Cooperative, Competitive, Hierarchical, Exchange, Affective, Epistemic); characterizes relationships along five dimensions (strength, type, reciprocity, trust, formality); analyzes relationship dynamics (formation, stability, dissolution, conflict); and surfaces relationship-level Hiddens (hidden dependencies, asymmetric reciprocity, betrayal risk, isolation). It populates the Social structure map, Evidence Ledger, and Risk Register with network-level analysis and relationship-dependent intervention assessment.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hidden Dependency | Relationship appears weak or unnecessary; actual causation hidden; failure of relationship creates cascade. | Dependency operates at long lag or in crisis mode; normal operation hides linkage; slack masks criticality. | Brittle system; cascade failure when relationship breaks; prevention opportunity missed. |
| Asymmetric Reciprocity | One party commits while other free-rides; expectations diverge; resentment accumulates silently until rupture. | Incentives diverge; one party has power to exploit; reciprocity assumed but not enforced; informal understanding breaks down. | Relationship rupture; escalating conflict; trust erosion; cooperation collapse. |
| Betrayal & Trust Erosion | Trusted relationship violated; expectations of loyalty or confidentiality broken; recovery difficult. | Trust assumed; monitoring low; betrayal highly consequential; deterrence weak; repair costly. | Relationship breakdown; cascading distrust; information suppression; isolation. |
| Coalition Instability | Alliance maintained by shared enemy; once enemy defeated or threat removed, coalition dissolves or realigns. | Coalition held together by external pressure not internal cohesion; incentive structure external not internal. | Unexpected dissolution; allies become adversaries; new conflicts emerge post-victory. |
| Relationship Asymmetry & Exploitation | Powerful party extracts increasing value from weaker; asymmetry grows over time; weaker party becomes trapped. | Power imbalance enables extraction; weak party has few alternatives; dependence increases; bargaining power decreases. | Increasing exploitation; resentment; vulnerability to shock; relationship becomes coercive. |
| Isolation & Redundancy Loss | Critical relationships concentrated in few individuals or channels; alternative relationships absent; system becomes fragile. | Efficiency optimization removes redundancy; trust concentrated; alternatives not maintained; path dependence. | Single point of failure; relationship disruption cascades; crisis response impossible; system collapse. |
| Conflict Escalation Spiral | Minor disagreement triggers defensive response; defensive response triggers escalatory response; conflict spirals. | Conflict generates negative sentiment; negative sentiment biases interpretation; ambiguous actions interpreted as hostile; defensive escalation is mutually reinforcing. | Rapid escalation; relationship rupture; third-party involvement; violent conflict. |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Dependency Invisibility (critical relationships appear weak until failure tests them)
- Asymmetric Reciprocity (one party exploits while other expects fairness; breakdown is sudden)
- Betrayal & Trust Violation (trusted relationships broken; recovery blocked by shame/rage)
- Coalition Fragility (alliances held by external pressure, not internal cohesion)
- Relationship Isolation (key relationships concentrated; redundancy lost; fragility hidden)
- Conflict Escalation Spirals (misattribution and defensive escalation drive rapid rupture)
- Power Asymmetry Concealment (exploitation hidden behind relationship narrative)

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Map focal relationships: list key dyadic, group, and organizational relationships; classify each by type (I–VI).
2. Assess 2–3 critical relationships: strength (strong/moderate/weak), type, reciprocity (balanced/asymmetric), and trust level.
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan: touch all six meta-categories; flag obvious hidden dependencies, asymmetric reciprocity, or conflict escalation patterns.
4. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence relationship risks (betrayal risk, isolation, coalition instability); note if relationship rupture or cascade risk is suspected; flag for escalation.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Relationship inventory: identify all focal relationships; for each, record type (I–VI), members, strength, reciprocity, trust, formality, history.
2. Relationship profile: for each critical relationship, assess along dimensions (strength, type, reciprocity, trust, formality); note asymmetries and gaps.
3. Dependency map: identify hidden dependencies; assess which relationship failures would cascade; test relationship criticality through scenarios.
4. Trust audit: for each relationship, assess trust level and trust vulnerability (betrayal risk, confidence gaps); identify fragile relationships.
5. Dynamics analysis: analyze relationship formation, stability, change trajectories; identify relationships drifting or at risk of rupture.
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-consequence relationship Hidden, identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; propose relationship-stability tests.
7. Network resilience: map network redundancy; identify isolated nodes or single points of failure; assess network robustness to relationship disruption.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add relationship-level Hiddens with meta-category tag (Cooperative, Competitive, Hierarchical, Exchange, Affective, Epistemic); mechanism; consequence. | After Tier 1 scan; escalate betrayal risk and critical dependencies. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record relationship types, trust levels, reciprocity patterns, dynamics; note evidence quality and gaps for hidden relationships. | After relationship inventory and profile analysis. |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about relationship stability, trust, dependency; propose relationship-stress tests. | Identified relationship Unknowns; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request relationship history and trust data; request network structure mapping; request relationship satisfaction/stability surveys. | To reduce relationship Unknowns; coordinate with Power and Social frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we mapped focal relationships, classified them (I–VI), assessed trust and reciprocity, identified hidden dependencies and asymmetries, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and designed relationship-aware interventions?
