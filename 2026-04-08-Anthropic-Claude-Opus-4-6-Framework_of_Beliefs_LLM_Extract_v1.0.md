## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Beliefs framework identifies and classifies beliefs shaping interpretation and action in any situation: espoused beliefs (stated) and operative beliefs (actually governing behaviour). It maps belief formation, entrenchment, and collective dynamics through six meta-categories and thirty core types, profiles five cross-cutting dimensions (scope, entrenchment, source, confidence, expressibility), traces ten belief dynamics patterns, and surfaces belief-driven visibility failures via tiered Hiddens scans. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, Hypothesis/Test Backlog, and Information Requests with belief-specific blindnesses and constraints on what solutions are thinkable.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Formation cascades | Belief clusters form in rapid succession; shared epistemic trust in single source leads to mimicry and adoption | Epistemic trust network expansion; mimicry; institutional adoption of source | Rapid formation can create shared mental models but also shared vulnerabilities to source corruption |
| Entrenchment acceleration | Beliefs become progressively more entrenched over time; identity fusion, dissonance resolution, persistence accumulate | Identity integration; rationalisation cycles; perseverance under challenge; motivated reasoning under threat | Accelerated entrenchment reduces revision window; entrenched beliefs generate espoused-operative divergence and defensive responses |
| Collective false consensus | Group moves from private doubt (Pluralistic Ignorance) to expressed false consensus (Groupthink); conformity pressure suppresses dissent | Fear of isolation; conformity pressure; leader signals; sanctions on dissent; psychological safety erosion | False consensus produces collective inaction despite individual agreement action needed; masks critical dissenting information |
| Espoused-operative divergence | Growing gap between stated beliefs and beliefs governing behaviour; agents express organisational line while acting otherwise | Conformity pressure; identity threat; retaliation risk; incentive misalignment; power asymmetry | Divergence enables concealment of operative dissent; produces misalignment between stated strategy and actual implementation |
| Belief drift | Beliefs shift slowly toward new equilibrium without triggering event; small degradations accumulate; norming of deviance | Accumulating small inconsistencies; incentives normalise deviation; monitoring gamed or ignored; reference point shift | Delayed recognition creates brittle systems; interventions become expensive and disruptive when noticed |
| Paradigm shift | Sudden wholesale reorganisation of dominant belief paradigm following anomaly accumulation or crisis event | Anomaly accumulation reaches criticality; alternative paradigm becomes available and credible; champion emergence | Enables wholesale belief revision that incremental challenge could not achieve; high consequence for decision-making during transition |
| Belief-action feedback | Belief generates action which produces outcome which reinforces belief; self-fulfilling prophecy or virtuous cycle | Self-fulfilling belief mechanism; confirmation bias in outcome interpretation; incentive structure supports interpretation | Can entrench false beliefs (negative self-concept → inaction → failure → reinforced) or sustain overconfidence |
| Source capture and corruption | Belief network dependent on single source becomes vulnerable when source is corrupted, loses credibility, or removed | Concentrated epistemic trust; source moves/dies/loses credibility/compromised; alternatives not equally trusted | Can produce rapid wholesale belief revision when source revealed as unreliable; or continued adherence if belief is now independent |
| Identity-protective entrenchment escalation | Beliefs fused with identity trigger increasingly defensive responses as identity is threatened; defensive responses strengthen rather than loosen entrenchment | Identity threat triggers defensive cognition; disconfirming evidence reinterpreted as attack; defence strengthens belief through rationalisation | Can make belief revision progressively harder as identity stakes increase; interventions attacking belief directly backfire |
| Collective paradigm boundary formation | Group develops shared paradigm boundary where certain hypotheses or evidence types are outside admissible inquiry frame | Paradigm formation through shared education/priors/institutional socialisation; boundary reinforcement through sanctions | Paradigm boundaries enable coherent group action but exclude consideration of alternatives; invisible to inhabitants |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | What operative beliefs are invisible to agents; what beliefs are tacit and unexamined; what beliefs govern behaviour without being articulated? |
| II (Systemic) | How are beliefs embedded in institutional structures (procedures, incentives, role definitions); what beliefs persist in structures independent of individual holders? |
| III (Informational) | What evidence-uptake mechanisms are filtering information (Confirmatory Processing, Motivated Skepticism, Paradigmatic Filtering); what do agents not see because filter rules exclude it? |
| IV (Temporal) | What beliefs are based on outdated information; what historical events continue shaping current beliefs; are beliefs tracking reality or persisting despite trajectory change? |
| V (Relational) | What beliefs are maintained through social enforcement, epistemic trust networks, or power asymmetries; is dissent suppressed through retaliation risk or psychological safety erosion? |
| VI (Ontological) | What paradigm boundaries make certain beliefs unthinkable; what background ontological commitments constrain belief space; what hypotheses are rejected a priori as inadmissible? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Espoused-operative divergence (stated beliefs differ from beliefs governing behaviour)
- Identity-protective entrenchment (beliefs fused with identity trigger defensive rather than deliberative response)
- Collective false consensus (private doubt masked by assumed majority agreement; Pluralistic Ignorance, Groupthink)
- Paradigm boundary invisibility (background assumptions treated as "how things are" rather than contingent choices)
- Confirmation bias and selective evidence uptake (beliefs filter what counts as admissible evidence)
- Belief perseverance despite complete evidential discrediting (entrenchment through non-evidential mechanisms)
- Epistemic trust network concentration (single source corruption creates wholesale belief collapse or persistence)
- Pre-theoretical commitment invisibility (framework inhabitants unaware of frame constraints on thinkability)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espoused-operative divergence detected | Framework of Governance; Framework of Hiddens (Tier 2) | Confidential interviews; behavioural observation; anonymous surveys; decision-outcome matching | Run theory-in-use elicitation; create safe-to-challenge protocols; align incentives; audit operative belief governance |
| Identity-protective belief entrenchment active | Framework of Values; Framework of Culture & Norms; Framework of Hiddens | Identity threat language detection; defensive affect observation; reframing design | Values-affirmation interventions; identity-safe framing; perspective-taking; psychological safety work |
| Pluralistic Ignorance or Groupthink suspected | Framework of Culture & Norms; Framework of Governance | Anonymous belief surfacing; gap analysis between private and public responses; fear language detection | Legitimacy of minority view; safe dissent mechanisms; norm-breaking permission; protected expression channels |
| Paradigm boundary limiting option space | Framework of Perspectives; Framework of Realities; Framework of Hiddens | Paradigm exposure through alternatives; admissibility rule testing; "what would falsify this paradigm?" probes | Paradigm-challenge sessions; perspective-taking with outsiders; option expansion; alternative scenario design |
| Belief perseverance despite evidence | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Diagnosis; Framework of Hiddens | Belief persistence testing after evidence discrediting; trajectory comparison (belief timing vs reality timing) | Requires paradigm-shift event or complete belief replacement; identity-safe new-belief environments; confrontation of underlying identity fusion |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. Surface focal beliefs through stakeholder interviews (espoused) and observation of decision patterns and behaviour (operative); note any obvious espoused-operative divergence.
2. Classify 3–5 highest-consequence beliefs using meta-categories I–VI and core types 1–30; note entrenchment signals (identity language, defensive affect, perseverance despite evidence).
3. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan across six meta-categories; check for perceptual blindspots (what operative beliefs are invisible), systemic embedding (structural belief carriers), informational filters (evidence exclusion rules).
4. Shortlist 2–3 highest-consequence Hiddens with mechanism and remediation interface.
5. Flag any high-consequence belief Unknown; assess escalation trigger (identity threat, retaliation risk, high consequence).

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. Create belief inventory through confidential interviews at multiple levels (frontline, middle, senior); distinguish espoused from operative through behaviour-statement contrast and theory-in-use probes.
2. Classify all identified beliefs (meta-categories I–VI, core types 1–30); profile each across five dimensions (scope, entrenchment, source, confidence, expressibility).
3. Identify collective beliefs (shared mental models, common knowledge, institutional beliefs, false consensus mechanisms); compare private and public belief statements (anonymous collection); assess psychological safety.
4. Map belief dynamics: trace formation mechanisms, entrenchment pathways, collective distortion patterns (Pluralistic Ignorance, Groupthink); identify espoused-operative divergence drivers; timeline any recent shifts or paradigm boundaries.
5. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each meta-category, identify high-relevance Hiddens; assess mechanism, detectability, agency, consequence; assign detection interface (how to surface) and remediation interface (how to address).
6. If applicable, invoke Targeted Hiddens Discovery Scans (e.g., Belief Persistence Scan for entrenchment, Positional Blindness Scan for role-locked beliefs, Paradigm Boundary Scan for pre-theoretical commitments).
7. Document residual Unknowns with mechanism and impact; apply Escalation Rule if Unknown could change decisions, accountability, or intervention priorities.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add belief-driven Hiddens (espoused-operative divergence, identity-protective entrenchment, false consensus, paradigm boundaries, epistemic trust concentration); meta-category tag (I–VI); mechanism; detectability; consequence | After Tier 1 scan; escalate high-consequence Hiddens to Tier 2 |
| Evidence Ledger | Record belief types, dimensional profiles, operative vs espoused gaps, evidence uptake mechanisms (filters, standards, confirmatory processing), quality of belief-evidence connection | After classification and dimensioning; note evidence gaps |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about belief formation sources, entrenchment mechanisms, collective distortion patterns; propose tests (theory-in-use interviews, anonymous belief surfacing, paradigm-challenge probes) | For all identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens |
| Information Requests | Request confidential access for interviewing; anonymous survey capability; decision-outcome data to assess operative beliefs; historical artefacts on belief formation timelines | To enable espoused-operative contrast and belief surfacing in safe channels |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we identified focal espoused and operative beliefs, classified them (meta-categories I–VI, core types 1–30), assessed entrenchment and formation mechanisms, detected espoused-operative divergence, run Tier 1 Hiddens scan across all six categories, and escalated high-consequence Unknowns to appropriate frameworks (Governance, Culture & Norms, Evidence, Hiddens)?

## Section 8: Cross-Framework Routing Pointers

### 8a. Upstream Inputs (frameworks commonly feeding into this one)

- Evidence (what is known, evidence quality standards, biases in available evidence)
- Perspectives (structured viewpoints, positional beliefs, role-shaped perception)
- Culture & Norms (social enforcement of belief expression, silence spirals, collective norm strength)
- Values (normative content, sacred values, identity-protective triggers)
- Agents & Personas (who holds beliefs, contexts, goals, mental models)
- Power & Incentives (who benefits from particular beliefs; what incentive structures reinforce them)
- Hiddens (what reality-relevant factors are suppressed, distorted, or invisible)

### 8b. Downstream Handoffs (frameworks commonly consuming this framework's outputs)

- Framework of Diagnosis (belief-architecture analysis underlying problems; belief constraints on thinkable solutions)
- Framework of Decisions (operative beliefs constraining option sets; belief-revision requirements for decisions to succeed)
- Framework of Interventions (belief-revision mechanisms and design; identity-protective constraints on acceptability)
- Framework of Governance (operative beliefs in incentive structures; espoused-operative divergence in implementation; belief-embedded structural change)
- Framework of Learning & Adaptation (belief-revision loops; paradigm shift requirements; operative belief update in learning cycles)
- Framework of Risk (belief-based failure modes; persistence despite evidence; entrenchment-driven brittleness)

### 8c. Targeted Scans (OG §7.5 scans that invoke this framework)

| Targeted Scan | Role of this framework |
|---|---|
| Belief Persistence Scan (Family A, Scan 4) | Identify entrenchment mechanisms when beliefs persist despite evidence or identity-protective dynamics are strong; deepens identity-protective entrenchment and perseverance typing |
| Positional Blindness Scan (Family B, Scan 3) | Surface beliefs locked to agent role or power position; reveal position-dependent blind spots in collective sense-making; contrast private and public beliefs by position |
| Paradigm Boundary Scan (Family D, Scan 5) | Expose pre-theoretical commitments and paradigm boundaries constraining option space; test admissibility rules; design perspective-taking to surface alternative paradigms |
