## Section 2: Operational Definition

The Power framework identifies who can impose constraints, shape options, and determine outcomes within a scenario; classifies power into six meta-categories and thirty core types; profiles each power base across five dimensions (Scope, Intensity, Visibility, Legitimacy, Mode of Exercise); maps five dynamic patterns (Accumulation, Circulation, Conversion, Resistance, Decay); and surfaces hidden, denied, or structurally embedded power that prevents accurate feasibility assessment and governance design. It populates the Hiddens Register, Evidence Ledger, and Hypothesis/Test Backlog with power-level blindnesses and strategic recommendations, enabling downstream frameworks to design interventions, governance, and accountability aligned with true power distribution rather than formal authority.

## Section 5: Condensed Dynamics

| Dynamic pattern | Signature (what you observe) | Mechanism (why it happens) | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accumulation & Concentration | Power holdings increase for already-strong actors; asymmetries widen; others' optionality shrinks. | Returns-to-scale, compounding resources, network effects, path dependence, capture of rules concentrate power. | Entrenched inequality; lock-in; widening helplessness of weak actors; signals need for anti-capture safeguards. |
| Circulation & Redistribution | Power shifts between actors after shocks, crises, reforms, or coalition changes. | Exogenous shocks, leadership change, disruption, scandal, policy shifts reallocate resources and legitimacy. | Windows for reform open after shocks; interventions can be mis-timed; incumbents lose control; contingency planning needed. |
| Conversion Between Power Bases | Actors convert one base (resources) into another (authority, legitimacy, network control). | Intermediaries, institutions, narratives translate resources into authority and authority into resource extraction. | Prevents one-dimensional analysis; shows how soft power becomes hard power; multi-base configurations more robust. |
| Resistance, Counter-Power & Constraint | Disadvantaged actors build counter-power; dominant power is constrained via law, norms, coalitions, sabotage. | Collective action, regulatory checks, competitive entry, narrative challenge restrain dominant actors. | Anticipates pushback and escalation; legitimacy management critical; escalation spirals risk if repression tactics used. |
| Decay, Erosion & Delegitimation | Power weakens as legitimacy, resources, network position erode; compliance drops; maintenance costs rise. | Overreach, poor performance, value conflict, transparency, or alternatives undermine acceptance. | Explains fragility of apparent dominance; compliance will fail; new configurations emerge; succession planning critical. |

## Section 6: Compact Hiddens Cross-Check Prompts

### 6a. Scan Questions by Meta-Category (I–VI)

| Hiddens meta-category | Scan question (1 sentence max) |
|---|---|
| I (Perceptual) | Which formal authority and gatekeepers are missing from the map; which subtle constraints are backgrounded as "technical"? |
| II (Systemic) | How do institutions and incentives systematically misrepresent power (theatre vs real authority, capture, gatekeeping)? |
| III (Informational) | Which power-relevant information is withheld, filtered, or distorted; where are accounts politically controlled? |
| IV (Temporal) | Which power configurations persist from history; what legacy arrangements remain; where is institutional forgetting active? |
| V (Relational) | Where is power so unequal that low-power actors cannot speak truthfully; where is retaliation risk high? |
| VI (Ontological) | Which power is emergent or structural and is misattributed to individual intent; what system-level agency is unrecognised? |

### 6b. Primary Hiding Mechanisms This Framework Detects

- Power Denial & Neutrality Illusion (treating decisions as technical while ignoring power)
- Surface Power Focus (visible authority only; missing network, narrative, and structural power)
- Static Snapshot Bias (missing dynamic accumulation, erosion, conversion, resistance)
- Unidimensional Reduction (reducing power to single basis; missing multi-base combinations)
- Hidden Principals & Shadow Structures (informal power operating outside formal channels)
- Positional Silencing (low-power actors cannot speak; elites misperceive constraints)

### 6c. Detection/Remediation Interface Pointers

| When this type of Hidden emerges | Invoke this framework | Interface type | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Formal process bypassed repeatedly | Framework of Governance; Framework of Causation | Shadow power mapping; informal network analysis | Identify real decision-makers; formalise decision rights; create transparent escalation. |
| Unexplained resistance to "sensible" proposals | Framework of Incentives; Framework of Narratives | Power-to-change audit; stakeholder impact analysis | Identify who loses from change; address legitimacy; reframe proposal; build coalition. |
| Frontline silence despite known problems | Framework of Communications; Framework of Risk | Protected disclosure audit; retaliation risk assessment | Create safe reporting; reduce power differentials; protect whistleblowers; independent oversight. |
| Persistent metric divergence from outcomes | Framework of Evidence; Framework of Governance | Distortion audit; incentive-power alignment check | Separate measurement from reward; diversify oversight; reduce gate keeper control. |
| Unequal policy impact across roles | Framework of Governance; Framework of Responsibility | Positional impact analysis; burden distribution audit | Ensure plural voices in design; test across positions; adjust for unequal exposure. |

## Section 7: LLM Execution Protocol

### 7a. Light Depth Execution (Scan-level)

1. List focal agents and decisions; identify who can block, veto, or approve each decision.
2. For top agents (3–5), identify visible power bases (formal authority, budgets, networks, expertise, coercive capacity).
3. Assess two dimensions: Visibility (Overt/Hidden?) and Intensity (Latent/Dominant?); note obvious asymmetries.
4. Identify which one power base dominates for each focal agent; note obvious conflicts (dueling authorities).
5. Run Tier 1 Hiddens scan across six meta-categories; shortlist top 2–3 hidden power holders or conversion pathways; flag for Tier 2 if high-consequence.

### 7b. Full Depth Execution (Complete framework run)

1. List focal agents, roles, and decisions; create power register template (six meta-categories × 5 types per agent).
2. For each focal agent, map power across all six meta-categories (I–VI) with provenance and evidence quality.
3. Profile each top power base across five dimensions: Scope (Local/Domain-Bound/Systemic), Intensity (Latent/Potential/Dominant), Visibility (Overt/Hidden), Legitimacy (Legitimate/Contested/Illegitimate), Mode of Exercise (Direct/Mediated/Structural/Symbolic).
4. Map all five dynamic patterns (Accumulation, Circulation, Conversion, Resistance, Decay); identify which are active; trace cascades.
5. For each focal agent, assess three interface types: Alignment (mutually reinforcing power bases), Contestation (power conflicts and tactics), Conversion (translation between power bases).
6. Run Tier 1 + Tier 2 Hiddens scan: for each high-relevance Hidden (Hidden principals, Shadow structures, Secret agreements, Positional silencing, Denial & neutrality claims), identify mechanism, detectability, consequence; assign detection and remediation moves.
7. Design feasibility-aware interventions; identify power constraints and anti-capture safeguards; document residual Unknowns; apply Escalation Rule.

### 7c. Register Updates and Gate Question

| Register | Update type | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hiddens Register | Add shortlist of power-specific Hiddens with meta-category tag, mechanism, visibility status, consequence, principal/agent. | Tier 1 scan completion; escalate any high-consequence/high-uncertainty Hidden to Tier 2. |
| Evidence Ledger | Record power classifications, dimensional assessments, dynamic pattern presence/absence, evidence quality, triangulation sources. | After power profiling and validation; note gaps (e.g., informal power evidence, network mapping, motivation of resistance). |
| Hypothesis/Test Backlog | Add hypotheses about hidden power, conversion pathways, counter-power emergence; propose tests (counterfactual vetoes, network analysis, access probes). | Identified Unknowns and residual Hiddens; escalation decisions. |
| Information Requests | Request governance documents, decision logs, org charts, budget data, network maps, interview access across power positions. | To reduce Unknowns and validate Hiddens detection; coordinate with Evidence and Governance frameworks. |

**Gate Question (pre-closure check):** Have we mapped all focal agents' power across six meta-categories, profiled each power base across five dimensions, analysed five dynamic patterns, assessed three interface types, run a Tier 1 Hiddens scan, and escalated any high-consequence hidden power (principals, shadow structures, conversion pathways) to Tier 2/3 or downstream frameworks (Governance, Interventions, Risk)?
